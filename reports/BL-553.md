### Why this is structural and not luck

The brief's hypothesis, inherited from BL-550, was that Apify is alive because
`TICK_HIKER_BUDGET_MS` (200s) is measured from the **cron tick start**, so earlier work eats HikerAPI's
budget and IG clips get handed to Apify unasked. **Measured over 7 days and 965 real Instagram batches,
that cannot be happening:**

| | measured |
|---|---|
| avg clips per IG batch | **8.8** |
| **biggest** IG batch ever | **26** |
| avg tick duration | 47.2s |
| **longest** tick in 7 days | **140.1s** |
| the budget | **200.0s** |

BL-550 measured a **1,268-clip** HikerAPI sweep taking **137.5s** at concurrency 10. A **26-clip** batch
is therefore roughly **3 seconds** of fan-out. **The batches are two orders of magnitude too small for a
200-second budget to expire**, and the longest tick ever observed still finishes 60 seconds inside it.

**So BL-550's hypothesis is refuted by measurement, and this is the round's real finding.** Apify was
never alive because of our own timeout. **It was alive because of the label bug and nothing else.**

**The honest caveat:** the longest observed tick is 140s, which is 70% of the budget. It has not crossed
200s in 965 batches over 7 days, but it is not structurally impossible on a pathologically slow tick.
`budget_abandoned` is now persisted, so if it ever happens it will be visible instead of invisible. And
I observed **2 fanout rows across 1 tick**, not a week. The 7-day batch-size argument is the strong
evidence here; the 2 rows are merely consistent with it.

**Verdict as the brief framed it: `budget_abandoned` is 0, so the remaining Apify traffic is genuine
misses, not our clock.** The next round is **not** the clock fix.

**Confirmed against the brief's specification:**

## For the owner: the query to keep watching

`budget_abandoned` is now permanently visible. Run this any time:

```sql
SELECT "occurredAt"::text AS at, platform,
       ("campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'total')::int           AS total,
       ("campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'primaryHits')::int     AS hits,
       ("campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'goneSkipped')::int     AS gone,
       ("campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'primaryMisses')::int   AS misses,
       ("campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'budgetAbandoned')::int AS budget_abandoned,
       ("campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'toApify')::int         AS to_apify,
       "campaignBreakdown"->'fanout'->>'invariantOk'            AS invariant_ok
FROM apify_usage_entries
WHERE provider LIKE 'fanout:%' AND "occurredAt" > NOW() - INTERVAL '24 hours'
ORDER BY "occurredAt" DESC;
```

**If `budget_abandoned` is ever > 0, the clock fix becomes real.** On today's evidence it will not be:
the batches are 8.8 clips and the budget is 200 seconds.

**Timestamps cast to `::text`** throughout, per the note that `run-select.js` renders
`timestamp without time zone` through a local +02:00 offset and once made the cron look dead for two
hours.
