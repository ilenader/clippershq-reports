## PART 3 — THE NUMBER THAT DECIDES EVERYTHING

### The rounding discovery

I probed the same live TikTok video through both TikHub endpoints within one minute:

| Source | play count | Divisible by 100? |
|---|---|---|
| TikHub `app/v3/fetch_one_video_by_share_url` → `play_count` | **16,466** | no |
| TikHub `web/fetch_post_detail?itemId=` → `playCount` | **16,500** | **yes** |
| Our stored value, polled 2h earlier | **16,300** | **yes** |

The two endpoints disagree by **34 views at the same instant**. And the statistical proof that our stored data is rounded, across the whole population:

| TikTok views bucket | Samples | Divisible by 100 | % | Expected by chance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1,000 to 10,000 | 11,235 | 99 | **0.9%** | ~1% |
| **10,000 and above** | **1,519** | **1,278** | **84.1%** | **~1%** |

**Above 10,000 views, 84% of our stored TikTok numbers are round hundreds.** That is TikTok's display rounding ("16.5K"), not a coincidence. Below 10,000, TikTok shows exact counts and our data is exact.

Instagram, by contrast: 1.0% divisible by 100 across 30,248 samples. **Instagram is not rounded**, which is why my IG probe matched to the digit (5,573 = 5,573).

### What this means, plainly

**BL-540's "exact match at 139,900" was a rounded value matching a rounded endpoint.** `web/fetch_post_detail` returns TikTok's *display* number; `app/v3` returns the *API* number. The 17-view gap it flagged was not noise. It was two different definitions.

**So the endpoint choice is a pricing decision, not a technical detail:**

* **`web/fetch_post_detail` → `playCount`** reproduces today's behaviour. Earnings do not move. You inherit TikTok's rounding forever.
* **`app/v3/...` → `play_count`** is more accurate and **changes what 84% of TikTok clips above 10k views earn**. Rounding to the nearest 100 is ±50, so the per-read effect is up to **±0.5% at 10k views**, ~0.05% at 100k. Small, systematic, and it is real money out of campaign budgets.

**Which matches today: `web/fetch_post_detail` → `playCount`.** That is the answer to PART 3's question. Whether you *want* the one that matches is the owner's call.

### The shadow proof that must pass before any switch

Specific enough to become a prompt.

1. **Build `tikhub-shadow`**, modelled on `src/app/api/admin/lamatok-shadow/route.ts` (which already works and already has a table: `scraper_shadow_samples`). Extend that table rather than inventing one; add a `tikhubViews` column and an `endpoint` column. **It writes nothing to `Clip.earnings`. Read-only by construction.**
2. **Sample 300+ clips per platform**, stratified deliberately, because the existing LamaTok run failed exactly by not doing this:
   * **View buckets: under 1k / 1k to 10k / 10k to 100k / over 100k.** The 10k boundary is mandatory — it is where TikTok's rounding starts, and the existing 10-sample run sits entirely below it and therefore proves nothing about the rounded cohort.
   * Ages: fresh (under 24h), 30-day, dormant.
   * States: public, restricted, private, deleted, geo-blocked. **The silent-zero cases are where a clipper's earnings freeze.**
3. **Run 14 days, not 7.** Both sources captured at the same timestamp, every tick. A 7-day run catches a definition mismatch; it does not reliably expose *lag*, and my own probe shows why: 16,300 stored versus 16,466 live is two hours of growth, not a vendor error. **You cannot tell lag from disagreement without a time series.**
4. **Pass criteria, fixed now, before anyone looks at data:**
   * **Exact match on ≥99% of live clips, computed separately for the under-10k and over-10k TikTok buckets.** A blended 99% would hide a total failure on the rounded cohort.
   * **Zero cases where TikHub returns 0/null while Apify returns a real number.** This is the earnings-freezing failure and it is disqualifying at any rate.
   * **No systematic bias:** median delta exactly 0, not "TikHub is 20 lower on average".
   * **Rate-limit budget: zero 429s** at full production volume. I hit a 429 in fewer than 10 requests.
5. **Pin the endpoint and the JSON path in code, with a comment naming this document.** Do not let a refactor swap `web/fetch_post_detail` for `app/v3`; they return different numbers.
6. **Switch one platform behind an env flag** with the shadow still running. Keep Apify warm a full cycle.
7. **Never backfill.** Historical earnings are settled (BL-534's era work); re-deriving them against a new view source moves real money.

### Step 1 — Fix HikerAPI's 25.7% failure rate. No vendor change. This is the whole prize.

You are already on the cheap vendor for Instagram. **8,272 HikerAPI calls, 2,128 failures, and every failure buys an Apify actor.** IG Apify spend is **$17.19/7d ≈ $74/month**, and it exists almost entirely to catch HikerAPI misses.

**Saving: potentially most of the $74/month IG line. Risk: LOW to MEDIUM** — it is diagnosis, not a switch; nothing moves until you know *why* it fails. **Nobody has looked at this.** Start here: bucket the 2,128 failures by classification (`HikerResult.classification` already distinguishes reel / carousel / unknown) and by whether the cooldown breaker tripped.

### Step 2 — Fix the ledger. Free.

The ledger bills only the winning provider and under-reports Apify by ~32%; four call sites never log at all. **Saving: $0. Value: you would have seen the cascade yourself.** Also re-price `lamatok-tiktok`, whose $11.30 is a fallback-rate artifact. **Risk: NONE.**

### Step 3 — Bios, profiles, submission validation (the SAFE, non-money jobs)

* **IG bio (#9) and IG profile (#8): TikHub can do both** and already does profiles in production. **Saving: ~$0.26/month. Risk: LOW.** Do it because it removes an Apify dependency, not for the money.
* **TT/YT bio (#10): UNTESTED on every vendor.** Probe before promising.
* **Submission validation (#1, #6 at submit):** same endpoints as tracking, so it rides along with the view migration. It is **not** a separate job.

### Step 4 — TikTok views. Money path. Only after the shadow proof.

**Two vendors are viable and one is already proven at small scale: LamaTok matched 7 of 8.** **Saving: the TT line, `legacy-tiktok-only` $7.79 + `none-tiktok` $1.03 ≈ $38/month.** **Risk: HIGH** — and the rounding question above must be answered first, explicitly, by the owner.

### Step 5 — Instagram views. Money path. Last.

Only the residue after Step 1. If fixing HikerAPI removes most of the fallback traffic, **there may be almost nothing left to migrate**. **Risk: HIGH**, and IG is the platform where the four-actor cascade exists precisely because scraping genuinely fails.

### What must stay on Apify

**Nothing proven, but two honest unknowns:** the **silent-zero / restricted-page fallback** (`apify.ts:176`) has no verified replacement — it exists because the *other* scrapers fail, and no vendor has been tested on restricted pages. And **TT/YT bio verification**. Everything else has a verified or already-live counterpart.

### If a vendor goes down

**This is the strongest argument against full retirement, and the brief is right to raise it.**

The cascade is not only waste, it is **redundancy**. Today: HikerAPI fails → Apify catches it → the clip still earns. **HikerAPI already fails 25.7% of the time and nobody noticed, because Apify absorbed it.** Kill Apify and that 25.7% becomes 2,128 clips per week with **no view update**, which means **frozen earnings** for real clippers.

Evidence this is not hypothetical: the LamaTok shadow shows a **HTTP 427** on 1 of 10 samples, I hit a **429** on TikHub within 10 requests, and `hikerapi.ts` ships a cooldown breaker because bursts of failure are expected.

**Recommendation: keep Apify configured as fallback-only, not retired.** The owner's stated goal — "Apify kept but never used" — is exactly right and is *already* the architecture for Instagram. Fallback usage is cheap precisely because it is rare. **The bill is high today because the fallback fires 26% of the time, not because the fallback exists.**

## PART 5 — THE VERDICT

**Can Apify be fully retired? No, and it should not be.** Keep it as a fallback. Its cost is a symptom, not a disease: **the Instagram bill is HikerAPI's 25.7% failure rate wearing an Apify invoice.** Retiring the fallback would convert 2,128 weekly failures into frozen clipper earnings.

**What would it actually save?** Apify is **$167.72/month measured** (BL-540, from Apify's billing API — not the $240 you believe, and I did not re-probe it because that number was already read from the authoritative source). A full TikHub migration lands near **$104/month, saving ~$64/month (38%)**. **But the honest ceiling is lower than that**, because Steps 4 and 5 buy the last portion at money-path risk, while **Step 1 (fixing HikerAPI's failures) is vendor-independent, carries no earnings risk, and targets the same $74/month IG line.** If Step 1 works, most of the saving arrives with no migration at all, and the remaining migration is effort and risk for the remainder. **So yes: say it plainly — the free fix captures most of the saving, and the migration adds risk for what is left.**

**The risk to clipper earnings is real and specific.** It is not "views might differ". It is: **84% of your TikTok clips above 10,000 views carry a display-rounded number, and one TikHub endpoint reproduces that rounding while the other does not.** Choosing `app/v3` silently reprices that entire cohort by up to ±0.5%. Choosing `web/fetch_post_detail` keeps today's numbers. **Nobody has been told this before and it is a decision, not a detail.**

### The single next step

**Diagnose HikerAPI's 2,128 weekly failures.** Not a migration. Not a shadow run. It is free, it is the 74% of the bill, it carries zero money-path risk, and it is the only step whose success makes the risky steps unnecessary. Bucket the failures by `classification` and cooldown state, and find out why one in four Instagram reads misses.
