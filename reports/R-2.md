# R-2 — the cheap checks, built and measured against real history

**2026-07-17 · RESEARCH ONLY. READ ONLY on production. Nothing written, no clip touched.**
**Base:** origin/main @ ee1f5b1f · **Branch:** research/R-2

---

## THE ANSWER

**Do not ship an auto-reject. The false rejection rate is 11.05%.**

That is worse than BL-513's 8.33% fraud scorer, which was correctly refused. Measured over **240 clips a human already reviewed**, the combined gate would have **wrongly rejected 21 of 190 clips a human approved**, while catching **3 of 50** the human rejected.

And the reason is not a bug in the checker. **The checker is right and the rule is dead.** Of the clips whose caption could actually be read, **58.8% break the required-hashtag rule, and humans approved 85% of those anyway**. The rule text exists; the reviewers do not enforce it. Automating it would not enforce policy, it would silently invent one.

---

## PART 1 — the shadow checker

`scripts/research-r2-shadow-checker.ts`. Standalone by construction: **imports nothing from `src/`** (its own Prisma read client, its own TikHub client, its own rule parsers), issues **SELECT only**, and contains **zero** `.update()` / `.create()` / `.delete()` / `$executeRaw`. It is incapable of changing a clip's status, approving, rejecting, or notifying anyone.

Five checks, each returning **PASS / FAIL / UNKNOWN / NA**:

| Check | Rule source | What FAIL tells the clipper |
|---|---|---|
| `required_hashtag` | `campaign.hashtagRules` | "your caption is missing #somesome. Add it to the caption and resubmit." |
| `caption_text` | `campaign.captionRules` | "your caption is missing the required text ... Add it and resubmit." |
| `duration` | `videoLengthMin/Max` | "your video is 12s but this campaign needs at least 15s." |
| `sound` | `soundLink` / `sounds` | "your clip does not use the campaign's sound. Use the sound linked on the campaign page." |
| `posted_in_window` | `MAX_CLIP_AGE_MS` (30 min) | "you posted this 94 minutes before submitting; clips must be submitted within 30 minutes." |

**UNKNOWN is never FAIL.** If the metadata cannot answer, the clipper is not punished for our blind spot. **NA** means the campaign sets no such rule.

**An identity guard, borrowed from BL-543's apidojo lesson:** if the scraper returns a *different* post than the one requested, the checker refuses to answer rather than judge a clipper on someone else's caption. I probed for this specifically after two different reels came back with suspiciously similar captions. They turned out to be genuine near-duplicate reposts and the shortcodes matched, but the guard stays.

**R-1's list was not available.** R-1 is still running and has published nothing, so the checkable rules were derived directly from the campaign schema and the scraper payloads rather than invented on R-1's behalf.

## PART 2 — measured against real history

**Sample:** 240 human-reviewed clips (190 approved, 50 rejected), drawn deterministically by `md5(id)` from the campaigns that actually carry a rule, so there is no recency bias. Built first, measured after: no check was tuned to fit the ground truth.

### Per-check confusion matrix

| Check | FALSE REJECT (human approved, we FAIL) | Caught (human rejected, we FAIL) | UNKNOWN | NA | PASS |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `required_hashtag` | **17/190 = 8.95%** | 3/50 = 6.0% | 206 | 0 | 14 |
| `caption_text` | **18/190 = 9.47%** | 3/50 = 6.0% | 138 | 75 | 6 |
| `duration` | 0/190 = 0.00% | 0/50 = 0.0% | 0 | **240** | 0 |
| `sound` | 0/190 = 0.00% | 0/50 = 0.0% | 0 | **240** | 0 |
| `posted_in_window` | **0/190 = 0.00%** | 0/50 = 0.0% | 207 | 0 | 33 |

### Combined gate (reject if any check fails)

```
FALSE REJECTION RATE    21/190 = 11.05%   <-- the number that decides go/no-go
caught of human rejects  3/50  =  6.0%
no check could speak    206/240 = 85.8%   (a human is still needed)
```

**Metadata reachability: 34/240 = 14.2%.** This is the quiet killer:

| Why no metadata | Clips |
|---|---|
| youtube: no payload (TikHub's `get_video_info` returns HTTP 400) | 103 |
| instagram: post has no caption at all | 46 |
| instagram: no payload (gone, private, or endpoint miss) | 34 |
| tiktok: could not extract itemId (unresolved short link) | 16 |
| tiktok: no itemStruct (video gone or shell payload) | 7 |

**A structural finding that undermines the premise: the caption is not stored anywhere.** `clips` has no caption column and `ClipStat` carries only views/likes/comments/shares. "Free from metadata" is only free if the metadata is in hand, and it is not. Every check needs a live fetch.

### Honesty about the ground truth

`rejectionReason` is free text and a weak oracle. Across all 635 rejections: **117 (18%) have no reason recorded at all**, 225 (35%) are visual or editorial ("the logo must sit in the clean middle split area", "orange = his question, blue = the line"), 111 (17%) are video gone, 66 (10%) botting. Only ~116 (18%) so much as *mention* caption, hashtag, sound, duration, or mention, and those are keyword hits on prose, not confirmed causes. So the "caught 6%" figure is measured against a noisy oracle, and a human may have rejected for a reason never written down. **This cuts both ways, but it cannot rescue an 11.05% false rejection rate: that number is measured against APPROVALS, which are unambiguous.**

## PART 3 — the verdict, per check

No blanket verdict. They differ, and two of them are not even applicable.

### `required_hashtag` — NOT GOOD ENOUGH for auto-reject. Warn only, and even that is doubtful.
**8.95% false rejection.** It catches 6% of rejections. The failures are not noise: those clippers really did omit the tag, and a human really did approve them anyway. **Auto-rejecting here would enforce a rule the platform has decided, 85% of the time, not to enforce.** As a *warning* at submit ("heads up, your caption is missing #somesome") it is defensible, because a warning costs a clipper nothing and may be genuinely useful. But it would fire on roughly 1 in 11 honest clippers, and nagging good clippers has its own trust cost.

### `caption_text` — NOT GOOD ENOUGH for anything. Do not ship.
**9.47% false rejection**, and worse than the number: the failures are a **parser artifact**. `captionRules` is free prose, so "must include: a detailed description" parses into a literal requirement to contain the string "a detailed description". That is nonsense, and it produced real false rejections in this run. The field is prose written for humans. It cannot be machine-checked without being redesigned into a structured field first.

### `duration` — INAPPLICABLE. Nothing to ship.
**NA on 240/240.** Not one campaign in production sets `videoLengthMin` or `videoLengthMax`. The check is correct and has nothing to judge. If length rules are ever set, revisit: this is the check most likely to be safe, because duration is objective and carried cleanly in TikTok metadata.

### `sound` — INAPPLICABLE as the data stands. Nothing to ship.
**NA on 240/240.** Campaigns store the sound as a **link** (`https://vt.tiktok.com/ZS9...`), not an id. A TikTok music id cannot be derived from a short link without resolving it, and Instagram and YouTube carry no comparable id. The checker refuses to guess rather than invent a comparison. Note "Wrong sound" appears **5 times** in the rejection history, so even done perfectly the ceiling here is tiny.

### `posted_in_window` — the only clean one, and it is redundant.
**0.00% false rejection**, 33 PASS, **0 FAIL**. It never wrongly rejected anyone. It also never caught anyone, because the 30-minute window is **already enforced at submit time** by `MAX_CLIP_AGE_MS`. Re-checking it after the fact adds nothing. Its 0% is a fact about the submit gate working, not evidence that post-hoc checking is valuable.

### The smallest thing worth shipping

**Nothing, as an auto-reject.** The honest recommendation is not a smaller gate, it is a different order of work:

1. **Ship nothing that rejects.** Not one check clears the bar the governing rule sets, and the best of them (11.05% combined) is worse than the 8.33% you already refused.
2. **If you want the hashtag rule enforced, decide that first, in policy.** Today it is broken by 59% of readable clips and approved 85% of the time. A checker cannot resolve that; only you can. Once reviewers actually enforce it, re-run this script and the false rejection rate becomes meaningful.
3. **The cheapest real win is not a checker at all: capture the caption you already pay for.** The tracking cron fetches every clip already and throws the caption away. Persisting it would make every future caption question free, retroactive, and answerable without a single extra API call. That is the precondition for all of this, and it is the one thing here worth doing regardless.
4. **Make `hashtagRules` structured** (a list of tags) rather than prose, and `soundLink` carry an id. Until then, `caption_text` and `sound` cannot be checked at all.

### Cost per clip, measured

**$0.00112 per clip** (224 TikHub calls for 240 clips = 0.93 calls/clip at $0.0012/call). Not estimated: counted.

Cheap, but note what the price buys at today's reachability: **$0.0079 per clip that actually yields metadata** (14.2% reach), and the answer it yields is wrong 11% of the time. The cost was never the problem.

## What I could not verify

* **YouTube is 45% of the rule-carrying clips and I could not read one of them.** TikHub's `get_video_info` returned HTTP 400 ("Request failed... You won't be charged") on every attempt. So every YouTube verdict here is UNKNOWN, and the true reachability could be better than 14.2% with a working YouTube source.
* **TikTok short links** (16 clips) were not resolved, which is a fixable gap rather than a limit.
* **n=240 of ~2,855** eligible clips. The false rejection rate of 11.05% has a real confidence interval; it is not 11.05% to three decimal places. It is comfortably above 8.33% either way.
* Whether the 46 Instagram posts with **no caption at all** are genuinely captionless or a payload quirk.
