## PART 4 — the migration plan

Ranked by risk-adjusted value. **Nothing here is built. This is a design.**

### Step 1 — Free, zero risk, do it first: stop the Instagram cascade

The cheapest win is not a migration. **Four IG actors bill on the same clip.** If the tier chain short-circuited properly, or the losing legs were removed, IG spend would drop without changing vendor. `apify/instagram-scraper` (the silent-zero fallback, `apify.ts:176`) alone ran **930 times in 3.5 days for $2.14**, purely as a fallback for the *other* IG scrapers failing.

**Saving: up to ~$19/3.5d of IG spend is spread across 4 actors; consolidating to one plausibly saves 40 to 60% of the IG line, roughly $30 to $50/month.** **Risk: MEDIUM.** These fallbacks exist because IG scraping genuinely fails; removing one blindly would turn paid retries into missing views, which freezes earnings. Diagnose *why* tier 1 misses before deleting a tier.

### Step 2 — Fix the ledger so the invoice is visible. Zero risk

The ledger bills only the winning provider, so it under-reports by ~32% and hid this entire cascade from you. **Saving: $0. Value: you would have seen this yourself.** **Risk: NONE** (logging only). Also add `logApifyUsage` to the four unlogged sites.

### Step 3 — Migrate TikTok view tracking to TikHub

**Saving: ~$6.69 per 3.5 days ≈ $57/month** (the whole TikTok line: clockworks + apidojo). **Risk: HIGH — this is the money path.** TikHub matched exactly on the one clip tested, via the *right* endpoint.

### Step 4 — Migrate Instagram view tracking to TikHub

**Saving: the remainder of the IG line after Step 1, roughly $50 to $110/month.** **Risk: HIGH — money path**, and IG is the harder platform (restricted pages, silent zeros are why 4 actors exist). Do this **after** TikTok proves out.

### Step 5 — Bios and profiles

**Saving: $0.03 per 3.5 days ≈ $0.26/month.** **Do not bother.** You were unsure whether TikHub is good enough for bios; the answer is that it returns a `biography` field, but **bios are 0.1% of your bill**. Migrating them is effort for a rounding error. Move them only when the IG account is already migrated for free.

### Must stay on Apify

**Nothing, on current evidence.** Every billing actor has a verified or plausible TikHub counterpart. The honest exception: **slideshows**, which you are not doing. If you start, test Apify against TikHub then.

### THE MONEY PATH: what must be proven before switching a single clip

Every clip's earnings are `(views / 1000) × clipperCpm`. **If TikHub's view number differs from Apify's by any amount, or lags, every clipper's pay changes silently.** My own probe already caught this: two TikHub TikTok endpoints disagreed by 17 views on one video.

**The proof, before any switch. You already have the pattern for this: `hikerapi-shadow` and `lamatok-shadow` are exactly this design.**

1. **Build a `tikhub-shadow` admin route** modelled on `src/app/api/admin/lamatok-shadow/route.ts`. It calls BOTH Apify and TikHub for the same clip and records both numbers. **It writes nothing to `Clip.earnings`.** Read-only by construction.
2. **Sample properly.** At least 300 clips per platform, stratified across view buckets (under 1k, 1k to 100k, over 100k), ages (fresh, 30-day-old, dormant), and states (public, restricted, private, deleted, geo-blocked). The edge cases are where a silent zero costs a clipper their money.
3. **Run for at least 7 days**, capturing both sources at the same timestamp, because a *lag* difference only shows over time. A source that is right but 6 hours stale still misprices a fast-moving clip.
4. **Pass criteria, decided before you look at the data:** exact match on ≥99% of live clips; zero cases where TikHub returns 0/null while Apify returns a real number (**that is the earnings-freezing failure**); and no systematic bias (median delta 0, not "TikHub is 20 views lower on average").
5. **Pin the endpoint and the field.** `tiktok/web/fetch_post_detail` → `stats.playCount`, and `instagram/v1/fetch_post_by_url` → `video_view_count`. Write down the exact endpoint and JSON path. **Do not let a later refactor swap to `app/v3` or `_v2`** — those return a different number and no number respectively.
6. **Switch one platform, behind a flag, with the shadow still running.** Keep Apify warm for a full cycle so a regression is one env var away from reversal.
7. **Never backfill.** Do not recompute historical clips against the new source. Era-boundary work (BL-534) means historical earnings are settled; re-deriving them with a new view source would move real money.

## PART 5 — the verdict

**What is burning the $8:** it is **$5.59/day, not $8**, and it is **99.88% actor calls**. **Instagram is 74% of it, TikTok 26%.** The driver is not the cron cadence (which is heavily throttled — 699 IG jobs poll at 72h). The driver is that **Instagram runs four billed scrapers per clip** — apidojo, api-scraper, reel-scraper, and a silent-zero fallback — and Apify charges for every one while your own ledger only records the winner. That is why the app thinks it spends $3.80/day and the invoice says $5.59.

**Eliminated immediately at zero risk: about $0.** There is no dormant Apify actor to delete. Everything billing traces to live code. The one free thing is fixing the ledger so you can see the cascade.

**Needs a migration: essentially all of it.** TikHub can replace TikTok (~$57/month) and Instagram (~$50 to $110/month) view tracking, verified returning **exactly the same view counts** on live clips. Realistic all-in: **~$168/month → ~$104/month, saving ~$64/month (38%)**. That is real money, but it is not $240, and anyone promising you $240 is quoting a premise that Apify's own billing API contradicts.

**What genuinely cannot leave Apify: nothing** on current evidence — except slideshows, which you are not doing.

**Your bio worry is unfounded twice over:** TikHub returns a `biography` field, and bios cost **$0.03 per 3.5 days**. It was never the problem.

### The one next step

**Fix the Instagram cascade before migrating anything.** It is the 74%, it is vendor-independent, and it needs no money-path risk. If you migrate to TikHub *first*, you will faithfully reproduce a four-call cascade as four TikHub requests and wonder why the saving is small. Diagnose why `apidojo/instagram-scraper-api` (359 runs/day) misses often enough to trigger `apify/instagram-scraper` (266 runs/day) as a fallback. Fix that, re-measure, **then** run the `tikhub-shadow` proof on TikTok.
