## The headline

Both defects are fixed, and the live ledger proves them. **`none-tiktok` holds 811 rows and 5,831 clips in 7 days: 68.9% of all TikTok clip-polls, recorded as served by NOBODY.** That is LamaTok's work. Meanwhile `lamatok-tiktok` shows 112 clips, 1.3%.

**That is BL-547's phantom, found.** BL-547 reported LamaTok at 1.4% and spent a round concluding the branch was dark and it must be a Railway env problem. The missing ~68.5% was never missing. **But I also found the brief's own invariant is arithmetically wrong, and caught my own fix inventing ~$81/week of fake cost before shipping.** Both below.

## PART 1: the attribution fix

### Defect 2 is not one hole. It is two different holes, and the brief predicted the wrong one.

`summarizeBatchProvider` read only `apidojoHits` and `legacyHits`.

**TikTok, exactly as briefed.** `lamatokHits` was computed at `apify.ts:1018` and thrown away by the very next return, which reported `apidojoHits: 0, legacyHits: 0`. A LamaTok-only batch therefore hit `if (s.apidojoHits === 0 && s.legacyHits === 0) return noneTag` and read `none-tiktok`.

**Instagram, NOT as briefed.** The brief says "the Instagram side has the identical hole for HikerAPI" and expects `none-instagram`. It does not happen, and the reason matters. At `apify.ts:1471` the code says so itself:

> *"2.C addition: legacyHits also absorbs Hiker hits — both are tier-1-equivalent successes for downstream metric continuity."*

`legacyHits = primaryMap.size`, and `primaryMap` holds Hiker's hits **plus** api-scraper's. So a Hiker-served IG batch has `legacyHits > 0` and was labelled **`apify-instagram-api-only`**. Hiker's work was not invisible. **It was actively billed to Apify's label.** Same family, worse symptom: silence would have been obvious, a confident wrong attribution was not.

**The fix:** `hikerHits` and `lamatokHits` added to `PlatformProviderStats`; the cheap primary is named FIRST, before the Apify-tier branches; and for Instagram `hikerHits` is subtracted back out of the tier-1 total (`apifyTierHits = legacyHits - hikerHits`) so "did a BILLED tier actually serve anything?" is answered honestly. `legacyHits` keeps its meaning for every existing consumer.

**Proof (the function is pure, so this is real proof, not a deploy promise): 8/8.**

```
PASS | TikTok served ENTIRELY by LamaTok      before: none-tiktok               now: lamatok-tiktok-only
PASS | TikTok mixed (LamaTok + clockworks)    before: legacy-tiktok-only        now: lamatok-tiktok+legacy-tiktok
PASS | IG served ENTIRELY by HikerAPI         before: apify-instagram-api-only  now: hikerapi-instagram-only
PASS | IG mixed (Hiker 700 + api-scraper 100) before: apify-instagram-api-only  now: hikerapi-instagram+apify-instagram-api
PASS | REGRESSION IG, Hiker OFF               before: apify-instagram-api-only  now: apify-instagram-api-only
PASS | REGRESSION TT, LamaTok off, apidojo    before: apidojo-tiktok-only       now: apidojo-tiktok-only
PASS | REGRESSION genuinely nobody served     before: none-tiktok               now: none-tiktok
PASS | REGRESSION undefined summary           before: none-instagram            now: none-instagram
RESULT: 8 passed, 0 failed
```

Four of the eight are regression cases: where the old label was already right, it is unchanged.

**Proof it is confined**, mechanically, not by assertion:

* **Exactly 2 functional lines.** Everything else in the diff is comment.

## PART 2: the fan-out counters are persisted

`hiker_hits`, `hiker_misses`, `gone_skipped` and `budget_abandoned` were computed at `apify.ts:1366` and thrown into a `console.log` that lives only in Railway. They are now on the ledger row, in the `fanout:` diagnostic row BL-547 designed, plus `primaryMisses` which BL-547 did not carry.

**`primaryMisses` vs `budgetAbandoned` is the whole point.** One means "the cheap primary was asked and could not serve". The other means "the cheap primary was never asked, because we ran out of time". BL-550 could not tell them apart and said so. That distinction is exactly what decides whether Apify's remaining traffic is a HikerAPI limit or our own 200-second timeout measured from tick start rather than fan-out start.

## PART 3: the invariant, and a correction to the brief

**The brief's invariant is arithmetically wrong. So is BL-547's header, identically.** Both state:

```
primaryHits + goneSkipped + toApify + budgetAbandoned === total
```

But at `apify.ts:1405` a budget-abandoned URL is pushed **into `missingForApify`**, and `toApify` is passed as `missingForApify.length`. **So `toApify` already contains `budgetAbandoned`.** Adding it again double-counts, and this invariant would have failed on every row where a clip was abandoned: precisely the rows that matter most. Had I implemented it as briefed, the first real abandonment would have raised a false breach and the number that decides Apify's fate would have arrived discredited.

Each URL lands in exactly one bucket, so the identities that are actually true are:

```
ROUTING:  primaryHits + goneSkipped + toApify === total
REASON:   toApify === primaryMisses + budgetAbandoned
```

Both are checked and `invariantOk` / `routingOk` / `reasonOk` are stored **on the row**, so a breach is queryable rather than asserted in a comment, plus a loud non-fatal `[FANOUT-INVARIANT] BREACH` warn. A breach is itself the finding, exactly as briefed.

**A budget-abandoned clip DID reach Apify** (that is BL-550's whole point: past 200s it is handed to Apify without HikerAPI ever being called), so it belongs inside `toApify`. It is not a HikerAPI failure, which is why `primaryMisses` and `budgetAbandoned` are kept separate underneath it.

**BL-547 was stranded and is now merged, not reimplemented.** `logBatchFanout`, `FANOUT_PREFIX` and the `$0` `estimateCost` guard were never on main: main moved to `ee1f5b1f` via BL-548 while BL-547 sat on `3af689a8`, exactly one commit ahead of a main ancestor. Merged (BACKLOG unioned), its `$0` guard preserved and extended. Nothing duplicated.

## The bug I introduced and caught before shipping

**My own fix would have invented roughly $81/week of fake Apify cost.**

The 8 new labels (`lamatok-tiktok-only`, `hikerapi-instagram-only`, and 6 mixed) were not in `PER_ITEM_USD` / `PER_CALL_USD`. `estimateCost` drops any unknown provider into the defensive worst-case branch: `callCount * APIFY_COST_PER_CALL_USD`, and `APIFY_COST_PER_CALL_USD = 0.10`.

So the moment 811 `none-tiktok` rows/week were **correctly** relabelled `lamatok-tiktok-only`, they would have gone from $1.05/week to **811 x $0.10 = ~$81/week of invented Apify cost.** The exact artifact this round exists to kill (`lamatok-tiktok` reading $11.20 for 112 calls), reintroduced by the fix for it, and it would have looked like Apify spend exploding at the very moment the round claimed to prove it collapsing.

**Fixed, and `$0` is the truth rather than a convenience:**

* **Cheap-primary-only labels go to `ZERO_COST_PROVIDERS`.** Two independent reasons: no Apify actor fires (when the primary serves everything, `urlsForApify.length === 0` and apify.ts skips the Apify call entirely), and the vendor's own cost is **already** recorded on its own rows (`hikerapi-v2` at $0.001/URL, 8,721 rows above), so charging the batch label too would **double-count HikerAPI**. Both vendors bill from a prepaid quota, not per-call dollars: BL-550 measured both balances unchanged across 2,153 Hiker and 407 LamaTok requests.
* **Mixed labels mirror the rate of the Apify tier that actually served.** These rates now land on the **right count for the first time**, because Defect 1 made `clipCount` mean "clips that reached Apify" instead of the whole pre-fan-out batch.
* All 8 added to `VALID_PROVIDERS` so `validateProvider` does not warn on a correct label.

**Proof: all 16 reachable labels priced, 0 hit the $0.10 branch.**
