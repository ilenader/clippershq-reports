## PART 2 — the top-up, traced with file:line

### 1 + 2. The resume mints a new era boundary (the owner's rule holds)

A budget increase on an AUTO-paused campaign hits `campaigns/[id]/route.ts:558-565` (requires PAUSED + pauseSource AUTO + `budget > oldBudget`), flips status ACTIVE nulling `pauseSource` and `lastBudgetPauseAt` (`:577-580`), and writes a `CampaignStatusChange` with `toStatus='ACTIVE', fromPauseSource='AUTO', triggeredBy='AUTO_BUDGET_INCREASE'` (`:626-645`). With `eraExempt` defaulting **false**, that row is a fresh era boundary matching `getCampaignEraBoundary`'s exact predicate (`campaign-era.ts:97-104`). So the default top-up freezes every pre-top-up clip. **BL-534's rule holds for somesome.**

### 2 (the tested question). A fifth boundary does NOT break the 328 PRR clips

`getCampaignEraBoundary` returns the **most recent** matching row (`findFirst` + `orderBy createdAt desc`, `campaign-era.ts:105-113`). Multiple boundaries coexist; the newest wins, and `isOldWindowClip = clip.createdAt < boundary` (`tracking.ts:1882`). **somesome already survives four boundaries.** A top-up mints a fifth (newest), moving the cutoff forward. Every pre-top-up clip, including all 328 PRR clips (all before 2026-06-22, hence before any newer boundary), stays old-window and frozen. **A second boundary does not break the PRR clips; it freezes them harder.** Only 4 somesome clips are currently newer than the 2026-06-22 boundary, holding $6.50 and 0 ambiguous rows, so re-freezing them costs essentially nothing.

### 3. The boundary alone holds the freeze after resume

Resume nulls `pauseSource` and `lastBudgetPauseAt`. That disables the budget-lock entirely (`tracking.ts:1961`, guarded by `budgetPauseAt` which becomes null) and disables the `isAutoPausedCampaign` arm of `campaignStatusBlocks` (`:1929-1934`). **But `isOldWindowClip` is independent of pauseSource** — it is driven only by the era boundary. So after resume, the era boundary is the sole thing freezing old clips, and it does hold: an old-window clip still makes `campaignStatusBlocks=true`, skipping the entire earnings block at `:1935`.

### 4. THE $934 QUESTION — traced exactly

`tracking.ts:2145` (`calculateOwnerEarningsGuaranteed(breakdown.clipperEarnings, sSnap)`, gross, no stamp-agreement check) sits **INSIDE** the `if (... && !campaignStatusBlocks)` block that opens at `:1935`. An **old-window** clip has `campaignStatusBlocks=true`, so the block is skipped and **line 2145 is never reached** for it. Therefore:

* **Default top-up (`eraExempt=false`):** all pre-top-up clips become old-window → gated out at :1935 → **never reach 2145** → the 108 ambiguous rows are NOT rewritten → the owner keeps the $933.94. Only clips created after the boundary (new money) reach 2145, and those stamp cleanly (see PART 3).
* **Exempt top-up (`keepExistingEarning=true` → `eraExempt=true`):** no boundary is minted, old clips stay current-era, campaign is ACTIVE → they DO reach 2145 → the owner's agency rows are rewritten to gross-via-s with no guard on the first ~5-minute tick → **the 108 ambiguous rows collapse from $1,928.71 to $994.77, a silent −$933.94.**

**So the $934 is not fired by the top-up itself; it is fired by the eraExempt flag.** The default is safe. The exempt option is the trap.

### 5. Views keep counting while earnings freeze

`ClipStat` is written unconditionally at `tracking.ts:1770/1782`, BEFORE the earnings recalc (`:1840`) and the gate (`:1929-1935`). Confirmed live: **372 somesome clips received a view update in the last 80 hours, the most recent 14 minutes ago**, while **0 of its 483 agency rows have changed in 7 days** (last owner-earnings write 2026-06-22). Views move; earnings are frozen. The client will see view counts update.

### 6. Cadence the client will see

Frozen clips poll every **4320 min = 72 hours** (`tracking.ts:283-292` AUTO_PAUSED, `:303-313` OLD_WINDOW). Live: **575 somesome clips are at 4320 min / AUTO_PAUSED**. On resume, the PATCH pulls every stale job's `nextCheckAt` to ~5 minutes out (`route.ts:592-600`) for one fast tick, but on that tick `getNextInterval` re-evaluates and hands old-window clips 4320 again. So the old clips settle back to 72h; only new-era clips get a fast view cadence. **The client watching the OLD clips will see a view refresh at most every 3 days.**

## PART 5 — the verdict

**Safe to top up today, on the default path.** The top-up mints a new era boundary (the owner's own rule), which freezes all 944 pre-existing clips including the 108 ambiguous rows and the 328 PRR clips, so `tracking.ts:2145` structurally cannot reach them and the $933.94 cannot be lost. Nothing must land first for the default path.

**The one way it goes wrong is the "keep existing earning" option**, which skips the boundary and lets 2145 rewrite the ambiguous rows for a silent −$933.94. Skipping the optional 2145 guard costs nothing as long as that flag is not used; using that flag without the guard costs $933.94. So the guard (item 6) is a real fix worth doing, but it is not a blocker for a careful owner.

Not structurally broken, not a manufactured blocker: the freeze machinery works, views count, the money is protected by the boundary. The single human decision is the rate (PART 3), and the single human discipline is to press the plain "increase budget" button, not the era-exempt one.
