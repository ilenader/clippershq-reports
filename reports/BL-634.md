# CLIPS_PER_TICK 30 to 60: health verification (BL-634)

**VERDICT: HEALTHY. 60 is live and safe. A 51 clip tick finishes in 12 seconds against the 210s graceful yield (about 17x margin); no overruns, no overlaps, no duplicate stats, no meaningful clip zeroed, cron firing every 10 minutes with zero timeouts. No rollback needed. (If a later reading degrades, roll back by setting CLIPS_PER_TICK back to 30 on the tracking-cron service.)**

Audit only, read only. Measured 2026-07-23 ~17:45 UTC from live prod data versus DB now(). No load test. Where a number could not be measured it is marked NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE.

## PART 1 : did it take effect? (empirical, not from the env)
Yes. Distinct clips per 10 minute tick (BL-633's SQL, cron only, isManual false):
• Through **07-23 15:00** the hourly maximum per tick was **27 to 30** (cap bound at 30, as BL-633 proved).
• **07-23 16:00 the first tick wrote 51 clips**, and 17:00 wrote 48. So the change **landed at ~07-23 16:00 UTC** (the owner's "a few hours ago" checks out).
• A tick can only exceed 30 if `clipsPerTick()` returned a value from the environment; the hardcoded fallback (`src/lib/tracking.ts:172`) caps at exactly 30. **51 > 30 is itself proof the cron service is now reading `CLIPS_PER_TICK` from its env, not the fallback.** The `[TRACKING-CONFIG] clipsPerTick=60 (source=env)` line (`src/lib/tracking.ts:3491`) would confirm the same in the cron service's Railway logs (NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE, but the per-tick count is the stronger, empirical proof).

Note: busy ticks read **48 to 51**, not a flat 60, because the tick is now **demand bound, not cap bound**. At the :00 hour rarely more than ~50 clips are due at once, so the cap of 60 is no longer the binding constraint. That is exactly the intended outcome: the tick polls everything due instead of leaving a capped remainder.

## PART 2 : is anything degrading? (before versus after)
Nothing is degrading. Every axis is flat or better.

• **TICK DURATION.** checkedAt span of the two big post change ticks: **07-23 16:00 (51 clips) = 12s, 17:00 (48 clips) = 12s.** At cap 30 a busy tick was ~7 to 12s. So doubling the tick size **barely moved the wall clock** (still ~12s), because the fetch is HikerAPI + LamaTok (fast), not the old slow Apify path BL-629 worried about. Margin: **12s against the 210s graceful yield is about 17x, and against the 300s route kill about 25x.** BL-629 projected 150 to 200s only at 120 with the old cost model; the real number at 60 is ~12s. Enormous headroom.
• **OVERRUN / OVERLAP / DOUBLE WRITE.** The cron heartbeat fired every 10 minutes after the change (16:01, 16:11, 16:20, 16:30, 16:40, 16:50, 17:01, 17:10, 17:20, 17:30, 17:40), **all kind="tracking" (zero "tracking-timeout"), all isBatchTick=true, no gaps.** **Zero clips were written twice in any tick window in the last 6h (max writes per clip per tick = 1).** So no tick overran, no two ticks overlapped, and no clip was double polled or double stat written. The single writer tracking lock holds.
• **CLIPS FALLING THROUGH.** Overdue active jobs now: **734 (521 in PAUSED campaigns, benign / earnings frozen; 213 in ACTIVE campaigns)**, versus BL-628's 07-22 baseline of 782 overdue / 568 over 12h (389 paused / 179 active). The overdue count did not worsen from the raise, but it also has not visibly drained yet because **the change is only ~1.5 hours old and most overdue clips are on 24h or longer cadences** that cannot recover in 90 minutes. The mechanism that drains them (larger ticks, 48 to 51 versus a capped 30) is now working, so the tail should shrink over the next 12 to 24 hours. Nothing is being skipped that was not skipped before.
• **ERRORS.** No cron timeouts since the raise (0 rows kind="tracking-timeout" since 15:55). Fast 12s ticks and the concurrency 15 HikerAPI semaphore mean no rate limit or budget overflow stress at 51 clips (51 fetches in ~12s is well under 15/s). Provider level error counts live only in Railway logs (NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE), but the heartbeat health and tick speed show no distress.
• **THROUGHPUT.** Direct evidence: **per :00 tick coverage rose from 30 to 48 to 51 clips, about +60 to +70 percent.** The hard ceiling rises from 30 x 6 x 24 = 4,320 to 60 x 6 x 24 = **8,640 polls per day**. A clean daily before/after cannot be computed yet (only ~1.5h post change; a rolling 12h "after" window is still mostly pre change hours and is additionally confounded by time of day demand), so the per tick gain is the honest, uncontaminated measure.

## PART 3 : money safety (freshness only, confirmed)
Confirmed: this is freshness, not money.
• **No meaningful clip was zeroed.** Zero clips whose maximum ever views exceeded 10 have a latest stat of 0 in the last 6h. So no clip with real views or earnings was zeroed.
• **The two clips reading 0 are pre existing noise, not the raise.** Both have **max ever views = 1** (near zero, oscillating 0/1 clips), written at 16:01; the identical pattern appears in the pre change window (1 such clip at cap 30), so it is baseline behavior, not caused by 60. Money impact of a 1 to 0 view move is (1/1000) x CPM, a fraction of a cent, effectively 0.
• **NULL never 0 holds** for every earning clip: a real low count of 0 for a 1 view clip is a correct reading, not a fetch failure writing 0, and no substantial count was overwritten with 0.
• **No duplicate stats** from any overlapping tick (0 double writes, PART 2), so no earnings double counting is possible.
• Earnings math is untouched by a scheduling cap change (BL-629), and the data shows no unexpected earnings movement.

## PART 4 : verdict
**60 is HEALTHY: a 51 clip tick runs in 12s against a 210s yield (about 17x margin), no overrun, no overlap, no duplicate stats, no meaningful clip zeroed, cron firing cleanly every 10 minutes.** 
**90 is safe as the next step:** extrapolating the measured ~12s per 51 clips roughly linearly gives ~20 to 25s for a 90 clip tick, still about 8 to 10x under the 210s yield. The only standing caveat is BL-629's: confirm the Railway compute budget has headroom first (the 2026-07-18 outage was a compute cap), though the flat 12s ticks and zero timeouts at 60 are early evidence that compute is not stressed. There is no reason to rush past 60; let it run a full day, confirm the overdue tail (especially the 213 active over 12h clips) drains, then step to 90.
