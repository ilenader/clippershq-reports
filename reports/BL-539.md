# BL-539 — What `isOwnerOverride` has cost the owner, platform-wide

**Date:** 2026-07-16 · **Base:** origin/main @ b9147a92 (post BL-536) · **Mode:** READ ONLY.
**Nothing was changed.** No code, no data, no schema, no money. One doc added. Nothing fixed.

**The number: $332.76.** That is the total owner share forgone across every campaign, all time,
computed with BL-162's GROSS formula. STRAENGE's $173.41 is **52%** of it, not the whole problem
and not a rounding error either. 85 clips, 14 clippers, 8 campaigns.

**The finding that matters more than the number:** there is **no evidence anywhere** that a single

believes it means.

## PART 0 — THE FLAG DOES NOT MEAN "NEGOTIATED ZERO-SHARE DEAL"

I went looking for genuine zero-share deals, because if any existed, flipping them would claw back
money the owner had agreed to give away. **I found none.** Four independent lines of evidence, all
pointing the same way.

### 1. The flag is set unconditionally. It is not a decision at all.

`owner-submit-core.ts:233`, inside the clip create:

```ts
status: "APPROVED", note: args.note || "Owner override submission", isOwnerOverride: true,
```

`isOwnerOverride: true` is hard-coded on **every** clip created through the owner-submit route. It
is not conditional on `customCpm`, not on a negotiation, not on anything. And what triggers override
mode has nothing to do with money (`owner-submit/route.ts:67`):

```ts
const isOverrideMode = Boolean(resolvedTargetUserId || resolvedTargetAccountId || postedAtRaw);
```

Submitting **on someone else's behalf**, or **backdating a post**. That is the owner's stated real
use case, encoded literally: *a clipper is about to miss the window, so the owner uploads for them.*

### 2. The CPMs are the campaign's standard rates. Nothing was ever negotiated.

The route accepts `customCpm` and caps it (`owner-submit-core.ts:188`:
`effectiveCpm = Math.min(customCpm, campaignCpm)`), so a discount is *possible*. It was never used.
Every override clip's stamped CPM equals the campaign's standard rate for its era:

The only clips whose stamp differs from the *live* campaign rate are on **somesome**, and those are
**era rates, not negotiations**. Proof: in the exact window the 2 override clips at $0.70 were

### 3. An owner CPM is stamped on all 99 of them. The system recorded share it then threw away.

**99 of 99** override clips have `ownerCpmAtSubmissionDecimal` populated, at the campaign's standard
owner rate. Submission recorded *"the owner earns 0.25 per 1000 views on this clip"* and then every
accrual path discarded it. A genuine zero-share deal would not stamp an owner rate. The code even
says so out loud (`owner-submit-core.ts:289`):

```ts
// Agency cut deliberately skipped — every clip here is isOwnerOverride (route L533-559, dead branch preserved).
if (!clip.isOwnerOverride && fullCampaign.pricingModel === "CPM_SPLIT" && fullCampaign.ownerCpm) {
```

The branch that would create the owner's row is **dead by construction**, and the comment knows it.

### 4. The free text says "the clipper couldn't post". Every single time.

81 of 87 live override clips carry the boilerplate note `"Owner override submission"` and a null
`overrideReason`. Every one of the handful with real text is a favour:

| overrideReason / note | Clips |
|---|---|
| `(null)` / "Owner override submission" | 81 |
| "he forgot to do it" | 1 |
| "cant verify app was not working" | 1 |
| "he didnt know his first video" | 1 |
| "OVA NECE DA SUMBITA" (Serbian: *he won't submit*) | 1 |
| "bogdan vid" / "Viraj" | 2 |

Not one mentions a rate, a discount, a waiver, or a deal.

### The flag DOES conflate two things, but not the two we expected

The real conflation is **provenance versus money**, and only half of it is wrong:

* **Provenance (correct, keep it).** `gamification.ts:221` excludes override clips from streak
  computation, and says why: *"a backdated override would retroactively repair a broken streak and
  grant unearned streak-bonus %."* That is a real, deliberate, correct rule. A backdated clip must
  not repair a streak.
* **Money (wrong, a non-sequitur).** From "the owner uploaded this, possibly backdated", the money
  paths conclude "therefore the owner earns nothing on it". Nothing supports that step. It was never
  a decision anyone made; it rode along with the provenance flag.

**Verdict: flipping these clips would not claw back anything the owner agreed to give away, because
no such agreement exists.** The $332.76 was never waived. It was dropped.

## PART 3 — THE PERMANENT FIX

### The books balance by construction. Here is the proof.

The budget already decomposes exactly: `budget = clipper pool + owner reserve`, where
`pool = (1−s) × budget` and `reserve = s × budget`. If **every** dollar in the pool accrues share at
`k = s/(1−s)`:

```
owner = pool × k = (1−s)·budget × s/(1−s) = s · budget = the reserve, exactly
total = pool + owner = (1−s)·budget + s·budget = budget, exactly
```

Measured on live data, not asserted:

| Campaign | Pool cap | Pool used | Owner if **all** clips accrued | Guarantee | Total | Within budget? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **STRAENGE** | $2,000.00 | $2,000.00 | **$1,000.00** | **$1,000.00** | **$3,000.00** | **Yes, exactly** |
| somesome | $6,543.62 | $5,452.06 | $2,671.51 | $3,206.38 | $8,123.57 | Yes |
| bees.n.honey | $1,648.35 | $1,066.66 | $874.66 | $1,351.65 | $1,941.32 | Yes |
| WinGram | $3,333.33 | $695.46 | $347.73 | $1,666.67 | $1,043.19 | Yes |

**STRAENGE lands on $1,000.00 and $3,000.00 to the cent.** The guarantee stops being a promise the
system cannot keep and becomes an identity it cannot break.

**BL-536's ~$3,346 warning does not apply to this proposal.** That warning was about *excluding
override clips from the pool cap* — a different and genuinely broken idea. The fix is the opposite:
**keep override clips inside the pool cap and let them accrue share.** The BL-167 clamp already holds
the pool at `(1−s) × budget`; once share accrues off that capped pool, total spend cannot exceed
budget arithmetically.

### Yes, split the flag

The flag is doing two unrelated jobs and only one of them is right. Split it:

* **Keep `isOwnerOverride`** as what it actually is: **provenance**. "The owner uploaded this,
  possibly backdated." Keep every gamification use exactly as-is. The streak rule at
  `gamification.ts:221` is correct and must not change.
* **Stop deriving money from provenance.** Who pressed upload says nothing about whether the owner
  earns their ordinary share.
* **Add an explicit `ownerWaivesShare` (boolean, default false)** *if and only if* a real zero-share
  deal is ever wanted. Today nothing would set it, which is exactly right: today, nothing means it.
  This keeps the capability without the current silent default of "waive everything, forever".

The argument for splitting is simply that the data proves the two meanings were never the same thing.
99 clips carry the flag; **zero** carry a waiver.

### What breaks: every path that reads `isOwnerOverride`

**Money paths that CHANGE (9):**

| Path | Today | After |
|---|---|---|
| `tracking.ts:2136` | `!isOwnerOverride` gates `useGuaranteeFormula` | gate removed; override accrues |
| `tracking.ts:2141` | legacy else returns 0 for override | accrues normally |
| `tracking.ts:2752` | **deletes** the agency row on override, every tick | stops deleting |
| `tracking.ts:2563` + `proportional-cut.ts:119` | override excluded from owner delta scaling | participates |

**Provenance paths that DO NOT change (8):** `gamification.ts:221, 414, 787, 855, 942` (streak and
level), `tracking.ts:2923/2925/2930/2969/2974` (level and stat sync),
`review/route.ts:1461/1467`. Override clips still must not repair streaks or inflate levels.
`tracking.ts:1958/3043/3620` are cadence and select plumbing, unaffected.

### Recommendation: ONE approach

**Let override clips accrue ordinary owner share, keep them inside the clipper pool cap, and split
the flag so provenance stops implying a waiver.** It is the only option that makes
`guaranteeOwnerSplit` true by construction rather than true by luck, and it needs no new reserve, no
top-up record, and no synthetic credit going forward.

**The honest downside, stated plainly:** including override clips in the BL-167 clamp means that on a
campaign whose pool is already full, a **future** override clip gets clamped, and the owner posting a
favour for a clipper will hand that clipper less than they expect. Worse, there is an existing
asymmetry this exposes: override clips currently consume the pool **without** being clamped, so on
STRAENGE they took $346.84 of pool space and the clamp squeezed **ordinary** clippers harder to make
room. Fixing this is correct but it is not free: it moves the squeeze onto the override clip itself,
which is the clip of the clipper who was already unlucky enough to miss the window. That is a real
tradeoff and the owner should make it knowingly. It is prospective only; no existing clip's earnings
change.

## PART 4 — THE VERDICT

**Total cost: $332.76**, all time, every campaign, 85 clips, 14 clippers. STRAENGE is $173.41 of it
(52%) and somesome $133.51 (40%); everything else totals $25.84. **91% is 4 clips held by 2 clippers.**
Under the legacy base-at-stamped-rate reading the total is $439.28 instead, a $106.52 spread that
hangs entirely on somesome's rate era.

**Is it recoverable? Mostly yes, which I did not expect going in.** This is the opposite of BL-536,
where the answer was "the money is not owed". Here it is owed, it was never waived, and the budgets
are mostly there:

* **$159.35 across six campaigns can be credited today.** They have thousands in headroom. Nothing
  blocks them but the decision.
* **STRAENGE's $173.41 is recoverable, in the right order.** Repair its stale rows first (owner down
  $47.10 to $826.31, unambiguous, zero stamp disagreements), then credit, landing at $2,999.72 of
  $3,000. Credit it naively instead and it breaches budget by $47.09.
* **somesome's $133.51 is blocked, and not by money.** It has $870.91 of headroom. It is blocked
  because 377 of its 483 owner rows carry a rate the locked share contradicts, and until the owner
  says which rate governs the old era, any figure computed for somesome, including that $133.51, is
  a guess with a $119.39 error bar.
* **Nothing requires reducing any clipper's earnings.** Not on any campaign. The forbidden case does
  not arise.

**The single next step: repair STRAENGE's 85 stale owner rows.** Not the override fix, and not the
platform-wide re-derive. STRAENGE alone, because it is the only campaign where the repair is
mechanical (zero stamp disagreements), it is the campaign where the money is largest, and it is the
precondition that turns a $47.09 budget breach into a $173.41 credit that fits. It needs its own
round and the owner's explicit sign-off, because it **reduces recorded owner revenue by $47.10**
before it increases it by $173.41.

**No optimism to manufacture, and one thing to be blunt about:** a blind platform-wide re-derive
would take **$932.49** off the owner, $889.03 of it from somesome's era ambiguity. That is not a
repair and it must not be automated. The order is: STRAENGE repair → STRAENGE credit → the six easy
campaigns → and somesome last, only after the era question is answered by a human.

### Confidence, and what I could not verify

**High** on the count and on PART 0. The forgone share is computed per clip from live data with the
formula the code actually has; the flag's meaning rests on an unconditional assignment
(`owner-submit-core.ts:233`), a trigger that never mentions money
(`owner-submit/route.ts:67`), 99 stamped owner CPMs, and 445 ordinary clips proving the somesome
rate was an era, not a deal.

Could not verify:

* **The 12 deleted and 2 rejected override clips** hold $0.00 and were excluded. If any were deleted

* **Whether `customCpm` was ever passed and silently clamped to the campaign rate.** The audit log
