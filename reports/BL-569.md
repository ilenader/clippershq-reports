## PART 1 — what it is, with file:line

`SCRAPEBADGER_API_KEY` is read in exactly two code files, both server-only:

| file:line | what it fetches | flow |
|---|---|---|
| `account-profile.ts:80,97,100,111,114` | avatar + follower count + verified, TT + YT | **avatar/follower refresh** (BL-415) |
| `creator-scan/providers.ts:63,95-140,142-171` | profile + up to 2 more calls (videos, comments), TT + YT | **creator scan** (BL-336 creator vetting) |

The exact calls:

* **account-profile TikTok:** `GET /tiktok/users/{handle}?region=US` → `user.avatar_larger`,
  `user.stats.follower_count`, `user.verified`. **One call per profile.**
* **account-profile YouTube:** `GET /youtube/channels/{handle}` → `avatar`, `number_of_subscribers`,
  `is_verified`. **One call per profile.**
* **creator-scan TikTok:** `GET /tiktok/users/{u}` **plus** `/tiktok/users/{u}/videos?count=20`
  **plus** `/tiktok/videos/{id}/comments?count=30`. **Up to three calls per scan.**
* **creator-scan YouTube:** `GET /youtube/channels/{h}` (includes `latest_videos`). One call.

**Two consumers, and only creator-scan is multi-call.** The avatar refresh is already one call per
profile. This distinction is the whole of PART 4.

## PART 4 — the one-call-per-profile design, and whether a new vendor is even needed

**The avatar/follower job is ALREADY one call per profile and ALREADY protected from the retry storm.**
`fetchAndStoreAccountProfile` (`account-profile.ts:161`) dispatches to exactly one per-platform fetch,
which is one SB call. And it fires **only on the APPROVED write** (`verify/route.ts:811-819`, inside the
approval branch), not on every verify click. **Approval happens once**, so a clipper clicking verify 21
times does not produce 21 profile fetches. The 21-retry storm BL-564 found hits `verify-cascade.ts`,
which uses **neither ScrapeBadger nor TikHub** (grep: zero references). So the retry storm and
ScrapeBadger are unrelated problems.

**If TikHub replaced SB here, it stays one call per profile by construction**, because the SB call it
replaces is already one call, on the same once-per-approval trigger. The design is a like-for-like swap,
not a new flow:

* **Cache:** the result is already stored on `clip_accounts` (`profileImageUrl`, `followerCount`) and
  refreshed on a **10-day** staleness cron (`refresh-account-profiles`, BL-415). Nothing needs adding.
* **Retry:** a re-verify does not re-fetch (fires only on the approval transition). A manual
  `refresh-profile` is rate-limited and role-gated (BL-415). The cron is 10-day-staleness gated.
* **What stops 21 calls:** the trigger is the approval write, not the button. Structurally capped.

**creator-scan is the only multi-call consumer**, and its expensive TikTok per-video calls are **already
failing to `videos_unavailable`** and already falling back to our ClipStat data. So dropping SB's TikTok
video calls costs almost nothing that is currently working.

### But the truthful answer is BL-564's: the profile job is not the problem worth spending on

The owner asked "can TikHub do everything ScrapeBadger does". **Yes, TikHub can do the TikTok profile
job in one call, proven above.** But the honest framing is that **the profile/avatar job is not what is
hurting anyone.** BL-564 measured the real pain as verification escalations (108 YouTube, TikTok bio
misses), and its highest-value fix is **not a scraper**: persist the fetched bio at attempt time, validate
the profile link at entry, and set the free `YOUTUBE_API_KEY`. None of that is ScrapeBadger, and none of
it is TikHub.

**So the two questions should be separated:**

1. **Replace ScrapeBadger** (the paid-now vendor): yes, cleanly. TikHub for TikTok profile (one call,
   proven), Google Data API for YouTube (free), our ClipStat for the already-dead TikTok video half.
2. **Fix verification escalations** (the real pain): that is BL-564's persist-bio + link-validation +
   free-YouTube-key work, and it does not touch either vendor.

Conflating them would spend TikHub quota to solve a problem TikHub does not have.

## PART 5 — the verdict

**What ScrapeBadger is:** a TikTok + YouTube profile scraper (avatar, followers, verified, and
creator-scan video stats), live in two flows, whose free tier has expired and now wants payment.

**Can it be dropped? Yes, and the replacement is cheaper or free per job:**

| SB job | replacement | cost | one call/profile? |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok avatar/followers/verified | **TikHub `fetch_user_profile`** | 1 TikHub call, cached 10 days | **yes, proven** |
| YouTube avatar/subscribers | **Google Data API** (already wired, `verify-cascade.ts:333`) | **free** | yes |
| creator-scan TikTok video stats | our own ClipStat (already the live fallback) | **$0** | n/a |

**Is one-call-per-profile achievable? Yes, trivially**, because the job SB does for avatars is already one
call on a once-per-approval trigger with a 10-day cache, and TikHub slots in like-for-like. The owner's
~2,000-calls-today concern is real but this adds at most **one call per account per 10 days** for TikTok
(≈306 accounts / 10 days ≈ 30 calls/day at steady state), plus the on-connect one-off. That is a rounding
error against 2,000/day, and it is capped by construction.

**The honest caveat:** replacing SB is a real code change to `account-profile.ts` and
`creator-scan/providers.ts`, not a config flip. And it is the *smaller* prize. The bigger one, per
BL-564, is the verification escalation fix, which needs no vendor at all.

### The exact ordered next step

1. **Check `YOUTUBE_API_KEY` in Railway.** Free, and it may fix YouTube profile fetches AND 108
   verification escalations at once. It is absent locally; if absent in prod, set it. Do this first
   because it is free and high-leverage.
2. **Swap ScrapeBadger TikTok → TikHub `fetch_user_profile`** in `account-profile.ts` and
   `creator-scan/providers.ts`. One call per profile, cached 10 days, proven to return the three fields.
   This is what stops the ScrapeBadger bill.
3. **Swap ScrapeBadger YouTube → Google Data API** (already imported in `verify-cascade.ts`), free, once
   the key from step 1 is set.
4. **Leave creator-scan's TikTok video calls as-is:** they already fall back to ClipStat and cost SB
   nothing that works.

After steps 1 to 3, `SCRAPEBADGER_API_KEY` becomes unreferenced and the bill goes to zero, with TikTok
profile work on TikHub at ≈30 calls/day and YouTube on Google's free quota.
