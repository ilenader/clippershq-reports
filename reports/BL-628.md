# SCALE CEILING — where Clippers HQ breaks first (BL-628)

Audit only, read only. Measured 2026-07-22 from live prod data, real config, and real provider limits. No load test was run against production. Where a number could not be measured it is marked NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE.

## TL;DR verdict
The platform is healthy on almost every axis to 10x and beyond. **One wall is close and it is the tracking cron.** It can process at most **4,320 clip polls per day** under today's config, and it is already delivering ~2,654 per day against ~4,028 tracked clips. That is roughly **1.5x to 2x of headroom, and less if new clips arrive fast** (new clips poll every 2 to 4 hours, ~10x the average clip). Everything else (providers, database, hosting, signup) is fine to 10x or 100x. The good news: the tracking wall is a **one env var change**, not a rewrite.

## Current real numbers (measured)
• Users: **1,204 total** (1,155 CLIPPER, 27 REVIEWER, 19 CLIENT, 3 OWNER). "~1,100 clippers" confirmed.
• Clips: **4,120 rows**, **3,299 approved** (BL-617), **~2,741 earning** live.
• Tracked clips (active tracking jobs): **4,028**.
• Tracking cron: fires **every 10 minutes** (6 ticks/hour) via the in process Railway scheduler; per tick cap **CLIPS_PER_TICK = 30** (measured: every busy tick in the last 24h processed exactly 30 clips); loop budget 210s inside a 300s route ceiling.
• Delivered throughput: **2,654 clip_stats written in 24h** across 1,784 distinct clips.
• Overdue backlog right now: **782 active jobs past their nextCheckAt**, of which **568 are >12h overdue** (389 in PAUSED campaigns, 179 in ACTIVE campaigns). The active ones are real earning clips being tracked late (no money lost, last known views are kept, but freshness lags).

## PART 1 — where the ceiling is, each measured

### 1. THE TRACKING CRON  ← FIRST WALL, and it is near
The cron collects at most `CLIPS_PER_TICK` due clips per tick, oldest nextCheckAt first, and defers the rest to the next tick. The cap is **30** (BL-197 lowered it 60 to 30 back when TikTok used the slow Apify clockworks actor at ~108s/batch). At 6 ticks/hour that is a hard ceiling of:

> **30 clips/tick x 6 ticks/hour x 24 = 4,320 clip polls per day.**

This ceiling is independent of HikerAPI concurrency. HikerAPI at concurrency 15 sweeps ~13.8 clips/s (BL-610), so 30 clips take about 4s of a 210s budget. The 30 cap is now **far more conservative than the time budget requires** because the fetch path is fast (Instagram via HikerAPI, TikTok via LamaTok). The cap, not the clock, is the wall.

Where it breaks: cadence weighted demand is ~1 poll per tracked clip per day on the mature mix, so 4,320 polls/day sustains roughly **4,300 to 6,400 tracked clips**. We are at 4,028 and delivering 2,654/day. **Headroom is ~1.5x to 2x.** Because a brand new clip polls every 2 to 4 hours (6 to 12 polls/day, ~10x the mature average), a wave of thousands of new clippers posting daily eats that headroom much faster than the steady state math suggests. **This is the wall that bites first, and it is the nearest one.**

### 2. PROVIDER RATE LIMITS AND DAILY BUDGET  (fine to ~100x technically)
• HikerAPI (Instagram): allowed rate **15 requests/second** = ~1.296M requests/day theoretical. Current usage **~1,115 calls/day**. Rate limit is not a wall until ~1,000x. The real constraint is the **prepaid request quota (~400,000 requests, BL-577)**: at 1,115/day that is ~1 year; at 10x it is ~36 days; at 100x it is under 4 days, so the quota must be refilled (a purchasing action, ~$280 per ~400k, not a technical wall). HIKERAPI_DAILY_BUDGET_USD defaults to $10/day and is **alert only** (BL-135.1), current spend $1.11/day, so 9x under the alert.
• LamaTok (TikTok bio + tracking): low volume today (tens to low hundreds of calls/day), priced $0.001 to $0.10/call depending on tier. Not a near term wall. Exact LamaTok account rate limit is NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE (no published ceiling in the code).
• Verdict: providers are fine to 10x, and to 100x with a bigger HikerAPI quota. Not the first wall.

### 3. THE DATABASE  (fine to 10x, watch two tables at 100x)
• Connection model: Supabase **transaction pooler (pgbouncer, port 6543) with `connection_limit=1`** per app instance, plus a direct URL on 5432 for migrations only. A prior production outage (2026-04-24) came from pointing DATABASE_URL at the direct 5432 port; the pooler is the fix and is correctly in place. A single app instance with connection_limit=1 uses almost no connections, so the pool is not the wall for a single instance. The ceiling is Supabase's pooler client limit and Postgres max_connections, both **plan dependent and NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE**; they matter only if the app is scaled to many instances.
• First query to degrade: the hot tracking reads (dueJobs order by nextCheckAt, and "last N stats for a clip") are **properly indexed** (`idx_tracking_jobs_active_interval_next`, `idx_clip_stats_clip_checkedat_desc`), so they scale to 100x. The fastest growing tables are **email_events (377k rows)** and **clip_stats (160k rows, ~2,654/day)**; clip_stats is the one tied to tracking volume and at 100x poll rate it grows ~100x faster, but stays fine as long as it is only read by the (clipId, checkedAt) index. The **apify_usage_entries** cost ledger (68k rows, one row per poll) is aggregated daily for the spend probe; give it a (provider, occurredAt) index before 100x if it is missing. None of these is the first wall.

### 4. HOSTING (Railway)  (single instance is the structural limit)
• The app runs as **one persistent instance** (the in process cron scheduler assumes it), 4GB Node heap (NODE_OPTIONS max-old-space-size=4096), route ceiling 300s. Exact Railway plan RAM and CPU are NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE, and the owner has already hit a compute cap once that took the site down, so headroom on the box is real but unquantified here.
• Structural point: **tracking is globally serialized by a lock** (acquireTrackingLock), so adding web instances does NOT raise tracking throughput. Web serving and signup CAN scale horizontally (pooler multiplexes), but the tracking wall must be moved with config, not instances.

### 5. AUTH AND SIGNUP  (not a bottleneck)
• Signup is magic link (no password): request is limited to **3/hour per email**, verify to **10/minute per IP**. These are per user throttles, not a global cap, so thousands of distinct people joining daily is fine. The real dependency is the **Resend email quota** for magic links (NOT MEASURABLE FROM HERE). Not the first wall.

### First wall, stated plainly
**The tracking cron. Hard ceiling 4,320 clip polls/day (CLIPS_PER_TICK 30 x 6 ticks/hour x 24). Delivering ~2,654/day today for 4,028 tracked clips. Saturates at roughly 1.5x to 2x current volume, sooner under fast new clipper growth.**

## PART 2 — the cost curve (measured, then extrapolated)
Real provider spend, last 7 days from the usage ledger:
• HikerAPI (Instagram): **$1.11/day** (~1,115 calls, ~$0.001/call).
• Apify Instagram tiers (api scraper + actor start + apidojo): **~$1.2/day** combined. **This goes to ~$0 once the BL-613 kill switch is flipped.**
• LamaTok + legacy TikTok: **~$0.7/day**.
• Total now: **~$3.3/day = ~$100/month**. Post kill switch (Apify off, BL-613): **~$1.8/day = ~$55/month**.

Blended cost per tracked clip post kill switch: ~$1.8/day / 4,028 clips = **~$0.0134 per tracked clip per month**. Extrapolating linearly (cost scales with poll volume, which scales with tracked clips, assuming CLIPS_PER_TICK is raised so the clips are actually polled):

| Tracked clips | Provider bill/month (HikerAPI + LamaTok, Apify off) |
| --- | --- |
| 1,000 | ~$15 |
| 10,000 | ~$135 |
| 100,000 | ~$1,350 |

Caveats stated honestly: (a) a young, active heavy population polls 3 to 5x more often than the mature mix, so early growth can run 3 to 5x these figures until clips age onto slower cadences; (b) at 100,000 clips the HikerAPI prepaid quota must be refilled continuously (a recurring buy, still linear in cost); (c) if the CLIPS_PER_TICK cap is NOT raised, spend is capped at ~$130/month no matter how many clips exist, because the cron physically cannot poll more than 4,320/day, which means the clips would barely be tracked. The cost curve above assumes you raise the cap to actually track them.

## PART 3 — what to fix first, ranked by when it bites
1. **RAISE CLIPS_PER_TICK (do this BEFORE growth).** The wall is a single env var. Raise it from 30 toward **~100 to 150** (it clamps at 500). At 100/tick x 6 = 14,400 polls/day (~3.3x today); at 150 = 21,600/day (~5x). Cost of the change: one Railway variable, no redeploy, no code. Validate that a tick still finishes under 210s (at ~13.8 clips/s a 150 clip fetch is ~11s; the per campaign Serializable write loop is the part to watch, which is exactly what BL-116-P3 capped for the slow Apify era that is now gone). This buys 3x to 5x immediately. Biggest lever, smallest change.
2. **Optionally shorten the tick interval 10min to 5min (before or during growth).** Doubles ticks/hour (6 to 12), doubling the ceiling again on top of the cap raise. Config only.
3. **Enlarge the HikerAPI prepaid quota (when tracked clips pass ~10,000).** Purchasing action, linear cost, not a code change. Watch the $10/day alert; raise it so it still means something.
4. **Add a (provider, occurredAt) index on apify_usage_entries if missing (before 100x).** Keeps the daily spend probe cheap as that table grows one row per poll.
5. **Confirm Railway box headroom and consider a second worker for non tracking load (when the compute cap is approached again).** Tracking itself cannot be parallelized by more instances (global lock), so this is about web/serving headroom, not tracking throughput.

Must do BEFORE growth: item 1 (and item 2 is cheap insurance). Wait until the numbers demand it: items 3, 4, 5. Do NOT rewrite anything; every fix above is a config or a purchase.

## PART 5 — the verdict
• **Maximum the platform handles TODAY:** comfortably its current ~1,155 clippers and low thousands more on the web/signup/DB/provider axes; on **tracked clips the practical ceiling is ~4,300 to 6,400 (hard poll ceiling 4,320/day), and it is at ~4,028 already**; daily submissions are gated per clipper per campaign (the 10/day cap), not a platform bottleneck.
• **First wall and its number:** the tracking cron, at **4,320 clip polls/day (CLIPS_PER_TICK 30 x 6 ticks/hour x 24)**, saturating at roughly **1.5x to 2x current tracked clips**, sooner if new clippers arrive fast. This is the only near term wall. Say it loudly: **tracking is the constraint, and it is close, not comfortable.**
• **Everything else is fine to 10x** (providers, indexed DB queries, per user signup throttles) **and to ~100x with a bigger HikerAPI quota and a cost budget** that grows linearly (~$15 / ~$135 / ~$1,350 per month at 1k / 10k / 100k tracked clips, Apify off).
• **Ordered fix list:** (1) raise CLIPS_PER_TICK to ~100 to 150 now, (2) optionally halve the tick interval, (3) enlarge HikerAPI quota past ~10k clips, (4) index apify_usage_entries before 100x, (5) confirm Railway box headroom for web load. Only (1) is urgent, and it is a single environment variable.

No load test was run against production, by instruction.
