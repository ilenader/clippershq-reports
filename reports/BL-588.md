## PART 4 — why the counter is too slow, and whether to lower K (recommend, do NOT change)
- **The K=5 window in real time.** A newly-dead clip escalates toward the dead cadence (INFRA_DEFER backoff
  to 6h, then DEAD_72H at 72h). At ~a gone-tick every 6-28h it takes **5 gone-ticks ≈ 3 to 6 days** to reach
  K=5, and it costs ~$0.01 (apidojo-IG) + ~$0 (api-scraper) per gone-tick, so **~$0.04-0.05 per newly-dead
  clip before the skip engages** IF it ever does.
- **But the reset makes K unreachable for most.** The counter resets on any real-stats resolve and increments
  only on a batch 404, so a flickering dead clip (the majority: 464/710 stuck at k=0) **never reaches K=5 at
  all** and is polled indefinitely. That, not the size of K, is the real leak.
- **Recommendation (provider-404-dead is reliable, unlike cadence-dead):** BL-584 proved a live HikerAPI 404
  is trustworthy (710 of 734 stayed 404; only 24 flickered, ~3%), and the skip fails open to NULL never 0, so
  a wrongly-skipped clip loses at most one view update, never a cent. So **K=3 is safe and K=2 defensible**
  for this PROVIDER-404 counter (BL-576's "22 of 30 alive" warning was about CADENCE-dead clips, a different
  and unreliable signal). Lowering K helps new deaths, but the higher-value fix is to **stop the counter
  resetting on a non-live resolve** (only reset on a genuine fresh-views resolve) and to **make retirement
  recurring**. Do not change K here; recommend it.
