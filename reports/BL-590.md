## PART 1 — fix the counter (non-money: gone-counter.ts + apify.ts)
- **Reset only on a GENUINE 200-with-views.** `classifyGoneCounterActions` now resets `consecutiveGone` only
  when the resolved stats object carries a numeric `views` (a real live resolve). A transient error, a
  timeout, an empty 200, a malformed payload, or a cooldown short-circuit produces no such object, so it can
  neither reset nor increment. This is BL-588's core defect: a flickering dead clip kept resetting to 0 and
  never climbed. Proven (unit): reset fires on `{views:1234}` only; NOT on `{}`, `{views:"oops"}`, `null`,
  or a never-asked clip; increment still fires on the definitive-gone set.
- **Lowered the skip bar K=5 to K=3** (apify.ts:662, non-money). Justification, stated in the code: this is a
  PROVIDER-404 counter (three independent HikerAPI /v2 404s), not the CADENCE-dead heuristic BL-576's "22 of
  30 alive" warned about. BL-584 re-probed 734 counter/cadence candidates live and 710 were still HTTP 404
  (the 24 revived were caught and left alone), so a definitive 404 is reliable. False-positive risk at K=3: a
  clip that 404s three separate ticks then revives loses at most ONE view update because the skip FAILS OPEN
  to NULL (never 0), so no earning is ever zeroed, and the next real 200 both resolves it and resets the
  counter. **What increments:** a definitive provider 404 in the batch fan-out. **What resets:** a real
  200-with-views. **What does neither:** transient / timeout / empty-200 / cooldown.
- **Honest limit:** the ideal "increment on the single-fetch 404 too" needs a change in `tracking.ts`, a
  frozen money file this round, so it is NOT done here. It is also made moot by PART 2, which re-probes the
  provider directly and never relies on the counter having climbed.

## PART 2 — recurring, safe, reversible retirement (new files; the proven kill)
`src/lib/retire-dead-clips.ts` + `src/app/api/cron/retire-dead-clips/route.ts` (CRON_SECRET-gated exactly
like /api/cron/tracking; `?dryRun=1` supported). It makes BL-584's one-off manual sweep automatic:
- **Candidate set:** live-polled Instagram clips (videoUnavailable=false, active) that are suspected dead by
  the counter (>=3) OR an escalated cadence (checkIntervalMin>=24h, BL-584's population). TikTok is excluded
  entirely (its ~$0.09/day is the honest floor and keeps polling).
- **MANDATORY fresh live HikerAPI re-probe per candidate.** Only a definitive HTTP 404 this run is retired.
  A 200 (alive) is left untouched and keeps polling; a transient/5xx/network probe is ambiguous and SKIPPED
  (never retired), so an outage can never mass-freeze. This is why the counter alone is never trusted (BL-584
  found 24 of 734 had revived).
- **Retirement = `videoUnavailable=true` + `videoUnavailableSince=now`, nothing else.** Never touches
  earnings (frozen at last-known, never zeroed), never `trackingJob.delete`, fully reversible. tracking.ts:1726
  auto-restores on any 200 and the counter resets on a real resolve, so a revived account un-retires itself.
- **Capped** at maxPerRun (default 100, hard max 500) and paced (1200ms/probe, BL-540's rate warning).
- **Dry-run proof (30 candidates, live re-probe):** candidates=30, reprobed=30, **confirmedGone(would
  retire)=6, revived(left alone)=24**, ambiguous=0, earnings that would freeze=$18.15. **Global earnings
  $9,371.58 before AND after the dry-run, unchanged.** The 24-of-30-alive result is the whole point: an
  escalated cadence is NOT proof of death; the fresh 404 is, and the re-probe caught the 24 live clippers
  that a cadence-only retirement would have frozen. Escalated IG candidate pool today: **805** clips, worked
  through at 100/run daily, each re-probed, only the fresh-404s retired.
