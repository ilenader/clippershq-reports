# BL-644 — The "fully spent" display rule, explained and judged

## VERDICT (one line): NOT A BUG. The rule is working exactly as designed, because the number on the clipper's screen and the number the earnings writer enforces are computed from the identical set of clips, so the bar can never advertise room the platform would refuse.

**2026-07-23 · DB now 20:35:49 UTC · AUDIT ONLY. READ ONLY.** Nothing fixed, changed or built. No code, data, schema or money change. Every figure below is from a live read with timestamps cast `::text`.
**Base:** origin/main `d6a5ca46` (**BL-641 has merged since the brief was written**, which changes PART 4) · **Branch:** checkpoint/BL-644 · isolated worktree, no node_modules junction. A markdown-only diff cannot change tsc or build, so none was run and none is claimed.

## PART 0 — the plain English explanation

### What the rule is
Every campaign has a budget, say $3,000. On the campaigns page a clipper sees a progress bar: how much of that $3,000 has been spent. When the bar is full, the campaign is out of money.

### Why BL-535 built it
On these campaigns the budget is split into two pots. One pot pays clippers, the other is the owner's share. On STRAENGE the clipper pot is $2,000 and the owner's is $1,000.

Here is the catch. **The owner's pot only fills as clippers earn.** So when clippers had earned their full $2,000, the owner's pot had only filled to about $874. Total spent, $2,874 of $3,000. The bar showed $126 still available.

**That $126 could never be earned by anyone.** The clipper pot was empty, so no clipper could earn another cent, and the owner's pot only grows when clippers earn. The money was stranded. But a clipper looking at the bar saw $126 left and reasonably thought "there is still money here, I will post a clip." They would have been posting for money that was already gone.

BL-535 fixed the display: **once the clipper pot is empty, show the campaign as fully spent**, 100%, no leftover advertised. It never invents a number and never names a campaign. It just asks one question.

### The question it asks
**"Have clippers already earned the whole clipper pot?"** If yes, show the bar as full. If no, show the real spend.

### What changed
On **2026-07-18** a bulk database update marked 419 clips as "video unavailable" (the video was taken down or went private). BL-638 documented this.

Now, the sum the platform uses for "how much have clippers earned" **skips clips whose video is gone**. So when those 419 clips were marked unavailable, their earnings dropped out of that sum. The number got smaller. And a smaller number is further from filling the pot.

**So the rule stopped saying "full" on campaigns where it previously did.**

### STRAENGE, with the real figures

| | |
|---|---|
| Clipper pot (the trigger) | **$2,000.00** |
| Clipper earnings the platform counts today | **$1,997.56** |
| Earnings on STRAENGE clips whose video is gone, not counted | **$4.15** |
| Counting those too, it would be | **$2,001.71** |
| **Short of the trigger by** | **$2.44** |

So STRAENGE sits **$2.44 below** the line that would make the bar read full. Before the sweep it was **$1.71 above** it. That is the whole causal chain: **video goes down, its earnings stop being counted, the running total drops, the total falls back under the trigger, the bar stops reading full.**

**What a clipper literally sees on STRAENGE today:** $2,998.10 spent of $3,000, a bar at **99.94%**, and a **$1.90** gap. If the rule fired they would see $3,000 of $3,000 at 100%. The difference on screen is **$1.90**.

### And here is why that is not a bug
The obvious worry is that the $1.90 is a lie, tempting a clipper to post for money that is gone. **It is not a lie.** The part most people miss is that **the earnings writer, the code that actually decides whether to pay a clipper, skips those same unavailable clips.** It uses the identical rule. So when those 419 videos went down, the platform genuinely stopped counting their earnings **against the pot as well**. The pot really does have $2.44 of room again, by the platform's own accounting, and the writer really would pay it.

**The screen and the payment logic agree to the cent.** That is the definition of an honest display.

### Could a clipper act on it? Traced end to end.
This is the question that settles the round, and it has two independent answers, both pointing the same way.

**First: they cannot even try.** STRAENGE is `PAUSED` with `pauseSource = AUTO` (verified live). The submit gate refuses a paused campaign outright at `clipper-submit-core.ts:97`, with the message "This campaign is paused, budget limit reached." And existing clips cannot grow either: `isAutoPausedCampaign` (`tracking.ts:1871`) feeds `campaignStatusBlocks` (`:1936`), which gates the whole earnings block. **Nothing on STRAENGE can earn another cent while it stays auto-paused, and the clipper is shown a paused campaign, not an open one.**

**Second, and this is the important one: if it were resumed, the $2.44 would be real.** The chain, verified line by line:

| Layer | File:line | Population it sums |
|---|---|---|
| What the clipper sees | `api/campaigns/spend/route.ts:63` | APPROVED, not deleted, **`videoUnavailable: false`** |
| What the pot cap is measured against | `balance.ts:311` → `clipperSideSpent` `:405` | APPROVED, not deleted, **`videoUnavailable: false`** |
| What the writer clamps on | `clip-earnings-writer.ts:344` reads `status.clipperSideSpent`, clamps at `:350-355` | the same value |
| The per-tick cron cap | `tracking.ts:2500-2508` | the same headroom |

**The display filter and the enforcement filter are identical.** So a clipper who acted on the bar would genuinely be paid, up to $2.44 clipper-side, before the clamp bit. **The display is not misleading. It is accurate.**

The failure this round was asked to look for, a bar promising money the platform would then refuse, **does not exist**. The two numbers are the same number.

## PART 3 — which definition of "spent" is correct?

The premise in the brief is that a retired clip's earnings were genuinely paid out, so the money is gone from the pot and ought to count. **On this platform that premise does not hold**, and the reason is decisive.

**A clipper cannot withdraw the earnings on a retired clip.** The withdrawal gate excludes them (`api/payouts/route.ts:411`, `videoUnavailable: false`, also at `:119` and `:162`). So that money never leaves the platform. It sits frozen on the row as a historical record.

That makes the platform **consistent on all three sides**:

| Question | Retired earnings counted? | Where |
|---|---|---|
| Can the clipper withdraw it? | **No** | `payouts/route.ts:411` |
| Did it consume the campaign pot? | **No** | `balance.ts:311` |
| Does the bar show it as spent? | **No** | `spend/route.ts:63` |

**Nothing was paid, so nothing is spent, so nothing is shown.** That is coherent, and it is the correct answer to the brief's question: for the exhaustion check, for the clipper bar and for the owner view, the honest definition is the one already in use, because it is the only one that matches what money can actually move.

**The one genuine inconsistency is on the owner side, not the clipper side.** The agency sum at `spend/route.ts:94` has no filter at all, so the owner's screen adds their entire accrual including amounts sitting on retired clips, while subtracting the clippers' retired earnings. BL-638 and BL-642 both documented this. Note that `balance.ts` already got this right for the cap math: BL-164 added a filtered `ownerAggApproved` (`:346`) used for `ownerSideSpent`, so **the enforcement layer is consistent and only the legacy display field is not.** That is BL-638's thread, not this one.

### What would move if the definition changed anyway
**Changing the display alone would create the very bug this round was looking for.** The bar would read full on STRAENGE while the writer still happily paid $2.44. That is a real phantom, manufactured where none exists today. It must not be done.

**Changing both display and `balance.ts` together** would be a genuine money-meaning change. It would shrink every clipper pot by that campaign's retired earnings, platform-wide **$3,530.30 across 567 clips**, and it would **reduce what clippers can still earn**: STRAENGE by $2.44, GainzAlgo by $71.70, WinGram by $19.07, somesome by $3,433.29 (moot, it is finished), Panic Baby by $2.09. On screen only STRAENGE would move, by $1.90. **That is an owner policy decision about whether a dead video should permanently consume budget, not a bug fix**, and it would take earnable room away from clippers on live campaigns.

## PART 4 — verdict, and how this sits with BL-641

### ONE LINE: this is the rule working correctly, not a bug that misleads clippers.

### Why the owner should leave it alone
1. **The screen matches the payment logic to the cent.** `spend/route.ts:63` and `balance.ts:311` filter identically, so the bar can never promise what the writer would refuse. This is the whole test, and it passes.
2. **The rule is behaving exactly as designed.** It asks a question about current state and answers it truthfully. State changed on 2026-07-18, so the answer changed. A rule that kept saying "full" after the underlying total dropped would be the broken one.
3. **The live exposure is one paused campaign and $1.90.** STRAENGE refuses submissions and has frozen earnings, so no clipper can act on it regardless.
4. **The obvious "fix" is actively harmful.** Adjusting the display alone manufactures a real phantom. Adjusting both layers takes genuinely earnable money off clippers and is a policy change, not a correction.

**One thing worth watching, not fixing.** STRAENGE sits $2.44 from its trigger. If it is ever resumed and clippers earn that $2.44, the rule fires on its own and the bar reads 100% with no intervention. Equally, if the 419 retired clips are ever restored (BL-638 flagged that their auto-restore cannot currently fire), the sums rise and the rule may fire again by itself. **Both paths self-correct. Neither needs code.**

### BL-641 and this rule coexist. Neither supersedes the other.
**BL-641 merged into main as `d6a5ca46` while this round was being briefed**, so this is now settled fact rather than a forecast. It added `isCampaignFinishedForClippers` (`campaign-clipper-view.ts:196`), reading `Campaign.status` and nothing else.

The two rules are **independent arms feeding one shared `Math.max`** at `resolveClipperFacingSpent:235-241`:

```
readsFull = budgetUsable AND ( isCampaignFinishedForClippers(campaign)   <- BL-641, asks "will the cron ever run again?"
                            OR isClipperPoolExhausted(...) )             <- BL-535, asks "can the writer still pay?"
```

They answer **different questions about the same underlying condition**, which is unreachable headroom. BL-535 catches a live campaign whose pot is full. BL-641 catches a campaign that is over. A campaign can satisfy either, both or neither, and because both funnel through `Math.max` the figure can only ever be raised, never lowered. **There is no conflict and no double-counting.**

**This is why somesome reads 100% today.** It was moved to PAST, so BL-641's arm fires on status alone. Its pot is $1,091.56 from exhaustion, so BL-535's arm was never going to fire and never did. **The owner should not read somesome's full bar as evidence that the pool rule works, and should not read STRAENGE's 99.94% as evidence that it is broken.** They are two different mechanisms, each correct, and BL-641 is the one currently doing the visible work.

Worth noting that BL-641 has, in practice, made this round's question smaller: the finished-status arm now covers all 5 PAST or COMPLETED campaigns regardless of their pot, so the exhaustion arm only has to handle live campaigns whose pot genuinely fills. Today exactly zero are in that state.

### If the owner disagrees and wants it changed anyway
Then the change is **not** to the display. It is to the shared definition, and it must move `balance.ts:311`, `clip-earnings-writer.ts` (via `clipperSideSpent`), `tracking.ts:2500` and `spend/route.ts:63` **together, in one round**. What must be proven: no clipper's own earnings figure changes anywhere (this is campaign-level display and pot accounting only), the 6 money files' behaviour is re-verified, and every campaign's displayed number is diffed before and after. Rollback is a single revert since no data would move. **This round does not recommend it.**

### What could not be measured, and why
• **Whether the 419 retired videos are actually gone.** BL-638 named this as the highest-value follow-up and it needs live probing of the URLs, which is outside a read-only DB audit. If they were wrongly retired, the honest fix is restoring them, which resolves this question by itself.
• **Whether STRAENGE will be resumed.** That is an owner decision and it determines whether the $2.44 ever becomes reachable in practice.
