# BL-635 — Is the campaign money model complete, to the cent?

Audit against live data (db now 2026-07-23 17:38 UTC), current merged main `6b0343a7` (BL-631 = BL-630 merged). READ ONLY: no code, data, schema or money change; no `--fix`, no re-derive, no repair SQL.

## VERDICT (one line)
The campaign money model is COMPLETE and exact to the cent for every path that moves money: a fee campaign's ghost, owner reserve and clipper pool sum to the marketed budget exactly and read marketed-fully-spent at exhaustion; the payout fee is taken exactly once at the correct rate on the correct base for all money that actually left or is in flight; every existing campaign is unchanged; the only residuals are bounded sub-cent rounding (a known population drift of exactly minus one cent, and a 7-cent clipper-favourable CPM-rounding display gap), plus one cosmetic display follow-up, none of which can overpay anyone.

## PART 1 — the full lifecycle, to the cent (merged code)
Modelled $5,000 marketed / 11% fee / 33% owner through the CURRENT merged helpers (`realBudgetFromFee` + the `balance.ts` cap formula), creation to exhaustion:
```
stored: budget $5000, platformFeePctDecimal 11.00, clipperCpm 1.0000, ownerCpm 0.4925, s=32.9983%
ghost $550.00 | realBudget $4,450.00
clipper pool cap $2,981.57 | owner reserve $1,468.43
AT EXHAUSTION: ghost 550.00 + reserve 1,468.43 + pool 2,981.57 = $5,000.00 (marketed $5,000)
spent (clipper+owner+ghost) = $5,000 of $5,000 -> reads MARKETED fully spent
```
`ghost + realBudget == marketed` is exact by construction (realBudget = marketed minus ghost); the three parts sum to $5,000.00 with nothing stranded. Nothing is left in `remaining` at exhaustion because the ghost is counted into `spent` the moment the campaign starts. The 7-cent gap between the calculator headline pool ($2,981.50, target 33%) and the runtime cap ($2,981.57, stored share 32.9983%) is the pre-existing BL-625 4-dp-CPM rounding, clipper-favourable and immaterial.

## PART 2 — the payout fee (not in the pool), on live data
The fee is `round2(requested * feePercent/100)`, feePercent 9 (or 4 referred), taken from the WITHDRAWAL not the pool, once, at payout creation, via the single shared `calculatePayoutBreakdown`; `finalAmount = requested - feeAmount - expressFeeAmount`. All three creation paths (`/api/payouts`, `/api/payouts/referral-request`, the dead `actions/payouts.ts`) share that helper. Across 133 payout rows: 0 fee-base mismatches (every feeAmount = round2(amount x feePercent/100)), 0 weird rates (all 9/4/0), 0 negative, 0 net-over-gross, express fee exact on all 27 express rows. No referred user was charged 9%. The clipper receives exactly `finalAmount`; the fee (feeAmount + expressFeeAmount) stays in the campaign budget as the platform/owner cut, reflected exactly as gross minus net. The 17 rows where `finalAmount != amount - fee - express` are ALL VOIDED historical rows (Mar to Apr 2026, feePercent null or a legacy 4%/9% field split), money never left; 0 PAID or in-flight rows mismatch. The campaign 11% ghost fee and the withdrawal 9%/4% fee are on distinct bases and never compound.

## PART 3 — the edges where cents hide
* ROUNDING: `ghost + realBudget == marketed` is always exact; the clipper pool cap and owner reserve are two INDEPENDENT round-half-up ceilings of realBudget, so at a tiny odd realBudget they can sum a cent over realBudget. That is a ceiling, not a payout: the L1 hard-lock (ghost-inclusive `spent` vs marketed, `clip-earnings-writer.ts`) bounds actual clipper+owner+ghost at marketed. Across the live population the earnings invariant `earnings == base + bonus` holds on 2,743/2,743 approved clips with a net drift of exactly minus $0.01 (clippers under-credited one cent total, the known cent, living in the still-Float earnings math, deferred F020).
* BONUSES AT THE NEW BOUNDARY: `tracking.ts` trims the clipper gross (base+bonus) to `clipperHeadroom = (1-s)*realBudget - otherClipperSpent`, BONUS FIRST. BL-630 only changed the cap BASIS from budget to realBudget; the truncation is unchanged, so a bonus cannot push a clipper past the new smaller pool cap.
* THE LAST CLIP: with $0.03 left and a clip that would earn $2.00, `tracking.ts` (`totalForThisClip > remaining`) ratio-caps clipper+owner to exactly the $0.03 remainder (guarantee path: to `clipperHeadroom`). Truncated to $0.03, never paid in full, never refused.
* MULTI-CLIP BATCH: tracking processes clips per campaign SEQUENTIALLY, each in a Serializable tx that commits before the next, so `spent` is fresh for every clip and the crossing clip truncates; the L1 hard-lock backstops. A batch cannot collectively overshoot (BL-627 proven, unchanged by BL-630).
