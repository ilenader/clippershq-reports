`apify.ts` already computed `urls.length`, `hikerHits`, `hikerGoneSkipped` and `urlsForApify.length`, logged them to Railway in `[SCRAPER-DECISION]`, and threw them away. **That is the exact gap that made three audits unreliable.** BL-545 nearly concluded from a spurious correlation that HikerAPI's hits were not preventing Apify calls at all, and had to admit the ledger could not tell.

### What I added

**`src/lib/apify-ledger.ts`** — `FANOUT_PREFIX = "fanout:"` and `logBatchFanout(...)`, mirroring BL-543's `logActorRun` shape exactly (same table, same prefix convention, direct write, never throws). One row per batch fan-out:

```jsonc

  primary: "hikerapi-v2",

```

**`src/lib/apify.ts`** — two fire-and-forget calls where the numbers already exist: the Instagram fan-out (after `urlsForApify = missingForApify`) and the TikTok LamaTok batch fan-out.

### Why this does not duplicate or double-count BL-543

They answer different questions and cannot collide:

| | BL-543 `actor:*` | BL-547 `fanout:*` |
|---|---|---|
| Question | which Apify actor billed, and what did it cost? | of N urls, how many did the cheap primary serve, how many were gone, how many reached Apify? |
| Cost | real estimate | **always $0** |
| Rows | one per actor run | one per batch fan-out |

**The $0 is load-bearing, not decoration.** I added an explicit guard in `estimateCost`:

```ts
if (provider.startsWith(FANOUT_PREFIX)) return 0;
```

Without it these rows fall through to the "unknown provider" branch and get billed at the **$0.10 legacy worst-case** — which is precisely the artifact that makes `lamatok-tiktok` read as **$11.40** (114 calls × $0.10) in the table below and that has misled three audits. **A diagnostic row must never be able to invent money.**

### The invariant it records

`primaryHits + goneSkipped + toApify + budgetAbandoned === total`. **Any row where those do not sum is itself the finding.**

**Constraint honoured: `tracking.ts` is untouched and byte-identical.** It holds 5 of the 10 `logApifyUsage` sites; BL-543 hit this same wall and solved it downstream of the caller. I did the same. Both files I changed (`apify.ts`, `apify-ledger.ts`) are non-money.
