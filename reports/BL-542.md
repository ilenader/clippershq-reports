### 1. The $933.94 somesome exposure — the biggest number, and NOT a hole to fix

Measured live: of the 1,247 owner rows on guaranteeOwnerSplit campaigns, **108 are rate-era ambiguous, all on somesome** (stored $1,928.71 vs gross-re-derive $994.77 = **+$933.94**). This is NOT over-accrual. somesome's ownerCpm moved 1.00 to 0.49 mid-campaign, so its stamped rate disagrees with the locked share and one locked share cannot hold two eras. **If somesome is ever unfrozen, `tracking.ts:2145` applies `calculateOwnerEarningsGuaranteed` to every guaranteeOwnerSplit clip WITHOUT the ambiguity guard, which would rewrite these 108 rows down and cut the owner $933.94.** That line is in a money file (`tracking.ts`), pre-existing, and the reason somesome must stay frozen. This is the exposure BL-563 flagged, now quantified. Do not touch it; it is the owner's era decision.

### 4. BL-164 phantom owner on FLAGGED clips — confirmed $92.17

Unchanged from BL-528: **3 FLAGGED clips carry agency rows worth $92.17** (owner over-accrued) and $113.50 of clipper earnings. Flagged clips are excluded from the cap math (BL-164's filtered `ownerAggApproved`) but still hold raw rows. This is the same category-inconsistency shape as BL-539: a FLAGGED clip is "bad" for the reviewer but "live" in the earnings and agency tables. Whether the $92.17 reaches owner P&L or the $113.50 reaches a withdrawable balance depends on whether the read path filters `status='APPROVED'`; the rows exist regardless.

### 5. Withdrawal overpayment — ~$1,113, but mostly by-design

Measured: 12 clippers have been PAID more than their **current** clip earnings, totalling ~$1,113 (top: fibonaccibojak $461.62, rhymes.22 $181.78, sl7r $144.02). This is larger than BL-528's $143.86 because it is a cruder measure that captures **every** reason earnings later dropped (clips deleted, rejected, or PRR-cut after a valid payout), not just PRR. Most of it is legitimate: they were paid for valid clips that were later removed, and the platform does not claw back from clippers (by design, clamped at $0). The genuinely-a-hole subset (post-payout PRR cut with no clawback) is a fraction of this and matches BL-528's order of magnitude. **Confidence medium: I could not cleanly separate "paid then clips removed" from "paid then PRR-cut" without the per-clip payout-funding history.** Nobody can withdraw NEW money from this; it is a sunk, non-clawed overpayment.

### 6. Stale PWA bonus — 365 clips, $165.19 bonus, PWA slice a fraction

Confirmed the definition still disagrees: `tracking.ts:2103` passes the raw `isPWAUser` flag (it does not even select `lastPWAOpenAt`), while force-recalc (`route.ts:253-256`) and cpm-restamp (`cpm-restamp.ts:122-125`) require PWA activity within 2 days. **365 approved clips carry $165.19 of total bonus while their user's PWA is stale (>2 days or null).** The PWA-attributable slice is only +2% of the clip's rate, so the actual stale-PWA exposure is a fraction of the $165.19 (roughly $15 to $33), latent, and path-dependent: whichever path last recomputed a clip set its bonus. Clippers gain a small amount.

### 7. Force-recalc raw-campaign caps bug — still present, BL-538 did not fix it

`force-recalc-earnings/route.ts:259` passes the RAW `clip.campaign` (live `minViews` / `maxPayoutPerClip`) into `recalculateClipEarningsBreakdown`, instead of building the snapshot-overridden `campaignForCalc` that `tracking.ts:2095-2102` uses. This trips the `earnings-calc.ts:256-289` tripwire that warns against exactly this. It calls `resolveClipEconomicFields` (route.ts:330) but uses the result only for `pricingModel`/`ownerUserId`, never for the caps. **BL-538 added the never-decrease guard around this line but left the raw-campaign call untouched.** Latent: force-recalc is admin-manual, so it only mis-prices clips when an admin runs it, using live caps against clips whose approval-time caps differed.

### 8. Marketplace — untested, and no Y1/Y2 caps in code

`calculateMarketplaceEarnings` (`earnings-calc.ts:629-691`) splits gross 60/30/10 with the only cap being `maxPayoutPerClip` on gross; the platform's 10% absorbs the rounding remainder and takes no bonus. **There are NO Y1/Y2 caps anywhere in the calculation** (grep is empty). **The entire path is untested by live data: 0 marketplace submissions, 0 earnings.** If Y1/Y2 caps are a policy, they are not implemented. Stated plainly rather than reassured.

## What is clean (measured, not assumed)

* **Rounding / the earnings invariant:** 3,184 approved clips, **0** rows where `|earnings - (base + bonus)| > $0.01`, total drift **$0.01**. No cents created or lost.
* **The platform fee:** 9% (4% referred), applied **once** at payout creation on the requested gross (`payout-calc.ts:83`), deliberately NOT deducted at balance formation, so it cannot double-subtract. Referral commissions and the owner/marketplace shares are not fee'd through the clipper payout path. (Doc nit: CLAUDE.md:88's "dedupes within 10s" is stale; the real guard is a pending-open uniqueness index, which is stronger.)
* **Payout idempotency:** pending-duplicate check + Serializable + partial unique index on creation; review flips and adjusts re-read status and are idempotent; `payoutReductionRatio` is an immutable multiplier. No double-creation path found.
* **No double-writes:** 0 clips carry more than one agency row.
* **In-flight payouts:** 5 (2 REQUESTED, 3 UNDER_REVIEW), each with a sound net (gross minus the 9%/4% fee). Two UNDER_REVIEW are stale (weeks old) — an ops backlog, not a money hole.

## PART 3 — category consistency

Only two clip categories carry any clipper earnings: **APPROVED** (1,243 clips, $9,228.92) and **FLAGGED** (3 clips, $113.50). No DELETED, REJECTED, videoUnavailable, or banned-user clips carry earnings, and no non-live clip carries an agency row except those 3 FLAGGED ones (#4). So the one category inconsistency is FLAGGED: excluded from the cap/review logic but still holding live earnings and agency rows on both sides. That is the BL-539 bug shape, small ($113.50 clipper + $92.17 owner), and the only instance of it in the population.
