# What the campaigns actually require, and what is already checkable for free (R-1)

RESEARCH ONLY, 2026-07-16. Read only: no code, config, data, or money changed. Nothing pushed to main,
nothing merged. Every number below is from the live database or a live probe.

**The blunt answer.** The single most common thing clients ask for is something a machine cannot see:
**8 of 10 real campaigns require something visual on the screen** (a logo placed just so, a card, an
overlay, burned-in subtitles, the product visible). And the largest real rejection cluster is exactly
that: **logo and overlay placement, 15.2%**. Meanwhile the rules that ARE free to check from data the
platform already fetches (sound, caption, hashtag, mention) account for **about 14% of rejections**.

So the hoped-for shape, "60% of rejections are wrong sound, automate that and go home", is **not what
the data says**. There is no single dominant cause. The free win is real but it is roughly a seventh of
the problem, not most of it.

## PART 1 — the real rules, from real data

### How rules are expressed: free text, and mostly not at all

All 30 campaigns in the database:

| Field | Campaigns with it filled | |
|---|---|---|
| `requirements` (free text) | **11 / 30** | 37% |
| `hashtagRules` | 3 / 30 | 10% |
| `soundLink` | 4 / 30 | 13% |
| `sounds` (jsonb) | 4 / 30 | 13% |
| `captionRules` | 2 / 30 | 7% |
| `bannedContent` | **0 / 30** | **never used** |

**The structured fields exist and are essentially unused.** The schema imagines a structured rule
engine; reality is one free-text blob, filled in for a third of campaigns. **19 of 30 campaigns have no
requirements text in the platform at all.**

**And the real rulebook often is not in the platform.** Four rejection reasons point clippers at
external Google Docs, verbatim:

> `read requriements https://docs.google.com/document/d/1fol6_ftyq2ueni0ttstjmah1g_qxfi9d/edit`
> `this is gainzalgo campaing read this https://docs.google.com/document/d/10f0pkzbhlgkjyqe7sr6_lb_9vpwk86oj/edit`

Any reviewer, human or machine, that reads only the database is reading a partial rulebook. **This is
the first thing to fix and it is free: the rules have to be in the system before anything can check
them.**

### What clients actually ask for, ranked by frequency

Across the 10 real campaigns with requirements (excluding the "Test" row, whose requirements are
literally "1 | 2"):

| Rank | Rule class | Campaigns | Checkable free? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **On-screen visual**: logo placement, overlay, card, burned-in subtitles, product visible, text pointing to the song | **8 / 10** | **NO. Pixels.** |
| 2 | **Caption content**: song name, credit, specific phrase, approved caption | 6 / 10 | **YES** |
| 3 | **Specific sound**, official only, no re-uploads or sped-up copies | 5 / 10 | **YES**, id compare |
| 4 | **Edit quality**: "highly edited", "cut to the beat", "old-money vibe", "no half done clips" | 5 / 10 | **NO. Subjective.** |
| 5 | **Posting window**, submit within 30 minutes | 4 / 10 | **YES, already enforced** |
| 6 | **@mention** a specific account | 3 / 10 | **YES** |
| 7 | **Hashtag** | 3 / 10 | **YES** |
| 8 | **Source footage**: her footage only, approved profiles, approved styles | 3 / 10 | **NO. Pixels.** |
| 9 | **No watermarks** (TikTok logo, other brands) | 2 / 10 | **NO. Pixels.** |
| 10 | **Platform restriction** (TikTok only, TikTok and IG only) | 2 / 10 | **YES, already known** |
| 11 | **Account requirements** | 1 / 10 | Partly |

**Rank 1 is the most-requested rule class and it is the one a metadata check cannot touch.** That is
the central fact of this research.

## PART 2 — why clips actually get rejected

**705 rejected clips, all time** (2026-04-23 to 2026-07-16). Of those, **558 carry a real reason** and
**147 are blank or null** (21%: a fifth of rejections record no reason at all, which is itself a data
gap: those clippers were told nothing durable, and no machine can learn from them).

### Clusters, ranked

Classified over all 558 non-empty reasons by keyword. **Caveat: first-match-wins**, so a reason
mentioning both sound and caption lands in sound. Treat the ranking as approximate, and the boundaries
as soft.

| Rank | Cluster | n | % | Could a machine detect it? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Dead link / video gone** | 113 | **20.3%** | **YES, already automated** (`videoUnavailable`). Not a rule violation at all. |
| 2 | **Logo / overlay / branding placement** | 85 | **15.2%** | **NO.** Needs pixels. |
| 3 | **View botting** | 67 | **12.0%** | **Partly.** Not metadata: needs view-curve analysis over time. |
| 4 | **Card content / on-screen text semantics** | 65 | **11.6%** | **NO.** Needs pixels AND judgement. |
| 5 | *Other / uncategorised* | 55 | 9.9% | mixed |
| 6 | **Caption / hashtag / comment** | 43 | **7.7%** | **YES, free.** |
| 7 | **"Read the requirements"** (unspecified) | 37 | 6.6% | **NO.** The reason records no rule. |
| 8 | **Sound wrong or missing** | 36 | **6.5%** | **YES, free.** |
| 9 | Test fixture (not a real rejection) | 24 | 4.3% | n/a |
| 10 | **Format** | 21 | 3.8% | Partly (duration/ratio yes; "quality" no). |
| 11 | Campaign paused | 4 | 0.7% | Already automated. |
| 12 | Wrong product promoted | 3 | 0.5% | **NO.** |
| 13 | Required element not visible (chart, signals) | 3 | 0.5% | **NO.** Pixels. |
| 14 | Edit / story structure | 2 | 0.4% | **NO.** |

### The honest arithmetic

* **Free metadata checks (sound + caption/hashtag/comment): 79 of 558 = 14.2%.**
* **Needs pixels (logo + card + element-not-visible + source footage): 153 of 558 = 27.4%.**
* **Not a rule violation at all (dead link + paused + fixture): 141 = 25.3%**, already automated.
* **Unattributable (blank reason + "read the requirements"): 184** counting the 147 blanks, **the
  single largest bucket in the whole dataset.**

**No cause is close to 60%.** The largest single real cause is logo placement at 15.2%, and it is the
expensive kind. **The cheapest genuine win is not automation at all: it is making rejections record a
reason.** A fifth record nothing, and another 6.6% say only "read the requirements".

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jul 13 (partial) | 201 | 174 | 11 | 94.1% |

**Roughly 400 to 660 clips reviewed per week by hand**, and the approval rate is **rising: 77.5% to
94.1%**. Latency from submission to decision, over 3,563 decided clips in 8 weeks:

| median | p90 | mean | max |
|---|---|---|---|
| **6.6 h** | **35.9 h** | 15.9 h | 793 h (33 days) |

**That is the prize: about 500 clips a week, a median 6.6-hour wait, and a tenth of clippers waiting
more than a day and a half.** Note the shape: **around 85% are approved**, so most of the human effort
goes into saying yes. An automated checker that could only confidently say "this one passes the
mechanical rules" would still remove most of the queue, and that is a far easier problem than judging
the rejections.

## PART 3 — what is already free, today

The platform already calls HikerAPI for every Instagram poll and LamaTok for every TikTok poll. **The
question is what those responses already contain.** Answer: **almost everything except the pixels.**

### Probes run, and their cost

**21 live calls total, all read-only, nothing written.** 11 HikerAPI (Instagram), 10 LamaTok (TikTok).
Five TikTok calls were wasted on my own wrong endpoint guess (`/v1/media/info/by/url`, 404) before I
read the client and found the real one (`/v1/media/by/url`, lamatok.ts:105). **Cost: HikerAPI ~$0.011**
(11 x ~$0.001, the per-call rate BL-540 derived from 1,180 calls/day at $1.18/day). **LamaTok's true
per-call price is unknown** and the ledger's $0.10 is an accounting artifact BL-540 already flagged;
even at that wrong rate 10 calls is $1.00, and the real figure is certainly cents. **Total: well under
a dollar, and under it on any honest reading.**

**Reliability note, measured:** on my first pass 2 of 5 Instagram codes returned **404** while 5 of 5
TikTok URLs returned 200. Both 404s were APPROVED, non-`videoUnavailable` clips from the last 10 days.

most HikerAPI "failures" are correct refusals on gone or private posts, but it means **a metadata
checker will not get an answer for every clip and must be able to say "I could not tell", never
default to reject.**

### Which PART 1 rules are free, right now

| Rule | Free? | Field |
|---|---|---|
| Specific sound / official audio | **FREE** | TT `music.id`; IG `clips_metadata.music_info.music_asset_info.audio_cluster_id` |
| Caption contains a phrase or song name | **FREE** | TT `desc`; IG `caption.text` |
| Hashtag present | **FREE** | TT `challenges[].title` + `desc`; IG parse `caption.text` |
| @mention present | **FREE** | TT `textExtra[].userId` (resolved, not just a string) + `desc`; IG `caption.text` / `usertags` |
| Minimum or maximum length | **FREE** | TT `video.duration`; IG `video_duration` |
| Posting window | **FREE, already enforced** | `taken_at` / `createTime` versus submission |
| Platform restriction | **FREE, already known** | the clip account |
| Caption edited after posting | **FREE (bonus)** | IG `caption_is_edited` |
| Account identity | **FREE** | TT `author.uniqueId`; IG `user.username` |

**Every one of these is at zero extra cost**, because the platform already makes exactly these calls on
every poll and throws the other fields away. Today the pipeline reads views and discards `desc`,
`music.id`, `challenges` and the rest.

**Against PART 2's rejection clusters, this free set covers about 14.2%** (sound 6.5% plus
caption/hashtag/comment 7.7%), and would have caught them **at submission instead of a median 6.6 hours
later**. That is the honest size of the free win: real, immediate, and not most of the problem.

## PART 4 — the honest gap

These rules **cannot** be checked from metadata, at any price, because the answer is only in the
pixels or in a judgement:

| Rule | Why metadata cannot do it | Rejections |
|---|---|---|
| **Logo / overlay placed correctly, fully visible, unobstructed, not "pasted on"** | The response says nothing about what is drawn on the frame. somesome's entire rulebook is this. | **15.2%** |
| **Card content and layout** (orange = his question, blue = the AI's line, card in the second half, never over her face, about 40% of screen) | Needs the frame read AND judged for meaning. WinGram's whole rulebook. | **11.6%** |
| **Product visible** ("Show the Crocs in every video. No Crocs, no pay.") | Object presence in frame. | in the 27.4% |
| **Text burned into the frame** ("Indicator name: GainzAlgo V2 Alpha" on screen the entire video; "text on screen must point to the song") | On-screen text is not in metadata. | in the 27.4% |
| **No watermarks** (TikTok logo, other brands) | Visual. | in the 27.4% |
| **Right source footage** ("use her footage only", "approved profiles list") | Needs matching against a reference library. | in the 27.4% |
| **Edit quality** ("highly edited", "cut to the beat", "old-money vibe", "no half done clips") | **Not even a pixel problem: a taste problem.** No tool decides this; a person does. | part of "other" |
| **View botting** | Not in the post at all. Needs the view curve over time, which the platform already stores in `clip_stats`. **A different problem with a different, cheaper answer.** | **12.0%** |

**The line, drawn plainly.** Free, today, from data already fetched: **sound, caption, hashtag, mention,
duration, posting window, account** — about **14.2% of rejections** and the mechanical part of **6 of
10 campaigns' rules**. Not free, needs a different class of tool entirely: **anything on the screen** —
about **27.4% of rejections** and the **most-requested rule class in 8 of 10 campaigns**. Not a tool
problem at all: **edit quality**, which is taste, and **view botting**, which is a time-series question
answerable from `clip_stats` without looking at the video.

**One thing that changes the economics and is worth recording here:** both providers already return the
**video file URL** (`video.playAddr`, `video_versions[0].url`) and the **cover image**
(`video.cover`, `image_versions2`). So the pixels are **reachable at zero fetch cost** whenever
someone does build that tool. That is a fact about today's responses, not a design. **This document
does not design that tool.**

## What could not be measured

* **Whether the free checks would actually have caught those 79 rejections.** I matched rule classes to
  clusters; I did not replay each rejected clip against its campaign's rules. That replay is the
  natural next research step and needs no new tooling.
* **The 147 blank-reason rejections and the 55 "other".** Nearly a third of the dataset is
  unattributable. The clusters above describe the 558 that said something.
* **LamaTok's true per-call price.** Unknown; the ledger's $0.10 is a known artifact.
* **The Google Docs rulebooks.** Four campaigns point at external docs I did not read. The in-platform
  rules are therefore a lower bound on what clients actually ask for.
* **`music.original = true` on an approved BAD BITCH ANTHEM clip.** Flagged, not adjudicated: I lacked
  the campaign's official sound id.

No build was run and none is claimed: this round's diff is this markdown file alone.
