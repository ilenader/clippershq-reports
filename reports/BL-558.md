## PART 2 — what actually fired, and why BL-529 does not govern it

### The alerts, from the notifications table (last 24h, and it repeats daily)

Every CRON_DOWN alert in the DB is one of **five** kinds, fanned to **three** owners, deduped once per 24h = **15 identical false alarms per day**. Fired 2026-07-15, 07-16 (14:00) and 07-17 (14:30), i.e. daily:

| kind | staleThresholdMin | per day | `lastHeartbeatAt` in the alert |
|---|---|---|---|
| marketplace-expire-deadlines | 30 | 3 | **null** |
| marketplace-counter-recompute | 10080 | 3 | **null** |
| marketplace-decay-strikes | 1500 | 3 | **null** |
| cleanup-magic-links | 1500 | 3 | **null** |
| notifications-cleanup | 1500 | 3 | **null** |

**Every one carries `"lastHeartbeatAt":null, "minutesSinceLastHeartbeat":null`** — that is the tell. These jobs have **NEVER heartbeat**, not "went stale". The email body reads "hasn't fired in ?? minutes" precisely because minutes-since-null cannot be computed. This is BL-526's exact finding reproduced: 5 dormant never-heartbeating jobs x 3 owners = 15 false emails.

**Proof they never ran:** `cron_runs` all-time contains only 4 kinds — tracking (7,655), lifecycle (213), watchdog (104), tracking-timeout (5). marketplace-*, cleanup-magic-links and notifications-cleanup have **zero** rows, ever.

### Why they fire: the code path, and why BL-529 does not stop it

`src/app/api/cron/watchdog/route.ts` on main iterates `CRON_KIND_THRESHOLDS` (a hard-coded list of 8 kinds) and computes `isStale = !lastFiredAt || lastFiredAt < kindCutoff`. **A job that never heartbeat has `lastFiredAt === null`, so `!lastFiredAt` is true and it is declared stale** — it fans out CRON_DOWN. There is no allowlist, no never-seen-versus-went-stale distinction, and no per-hour rate limit on main; only a 24h per-(kind,owner) dedup, which is why the storm is exactly 15/day rather than continuous.

**BL-529 is NOT deployed. This is the central finding, and the brief's premise that it "is live, merged via BL-546" is false, verified three ways:**

1. `git merge-base --is-ancestor e34ac3fc origin/main` returns **NO** (e34ac3fc is "BL-529: fix the cron watchdog alert storm").
2. `origin/checkpoint/BL-529` exists @ c1f102fd but `merge-base --is-ancestor origin/checkpoint/BL-529 origin/main` returns **NO**.
3. There is **no "Merge BL-546" commit** in history, and the watchdog route's last change on main is **BL-410**, with no BL-526/529/546 touching it. `cron-watchdog-policy.ts` (which the brief named) **does not exist on main**, and `RAILWAY_NATIVE_CRON` appears only in `railway-cron-scheduler.ts`, never in the watchdog.

BL-529's branch watchdog carries the full fix (`resolveWatchedKinds(process.env.RAILWAY_NATIVE_CRON)` = allowlist PLUS tracking; severity tiers where tracking alone emails; never-seen vs went-stale). Its **own code comment** names the cause: *"heartbeats all-time because nothing fires them (RAILWAY_NATIVE_CRON lists only lifecycle + watchdog). The pre-BL-529 CRON_KIND_THRESHOLDS constant that drove [the storm]..."*. So the fix exists, describes this exact bug, and is one merge away.

**The money cron stays watched on main regardless:** `"tracking": 45` is hard-coded in `CRON_KIND_THRESHOLDS`, and BL-529's design also watches tracking for every allowlist value. Neither the current bug nor the fix can unwatch the money cron. Verified on current main.
