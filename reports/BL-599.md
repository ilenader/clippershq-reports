## WHAT WAS BUILT (and the absolute rule)

A NEW, independent signal that compares a clip's like-rate (likes/views) to the NORMAL band for its platform and niche, computed from live data, and fires on BOTH suspiciously HIGH engagement (too many likes for the views, the owner's 70-views / 11-likes case) and suspiciously LOW engagement (bought views that bring no likes). It **RANKS a clip for the owner's eyes**. It never rejects, never freezes, never changes a clip's status, never touches earnings or tracking, and the clipper never sees any of it (BL-518). R-5 proved the old aggregate `fraudScore` has no predictive power, so this is not built on it: it is a separate signal that earns trust by ranking, not gating.

Files:
* `src/lib/peer-engagement.ts` (pure, no DB, no network, never mutates): the signal.
* `src/lib/peer-engagement-bands.generated.ts`: the bands, baked from live data (3195 clips). Regenerate any time with `node scripts/gen-peer-bands.mjs`.
* `src/app/api/admin/fraud-review/route.ts` + `.../admin/fraud-review/page.tsx`: surface it, read-only, owner-only.
* `scripts/bl599-verify.ts`: the read-only validation that produced every number below.

## PART 1: THE PEER-RELATIVE SIGNAL

### The bands, from live data (not guessed)

Like-rate of the latest snapshot of every APPROVED, non-deleted clip with views over 0. Platform level (the fallback) and a niche level kept only at 40+ clips:

| peer group | clips | p50 like-rate | p90 like-rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok (platform) | 837 | 4.18% | 11.86% |
| Instagram (platform) | 1285 | 1.02% | 6.18% |
| YouTube (platform) | 1073 | 0.19% | 9.09% |
| Instagram / Facts (niche) | 94 | 0.40% | 0.76% |
| TikTok / Relatable (niche) | 90 | 8.31% | 12.69% |
| Instagram / Comedy, memes (niche) | 42 | 0.07% | 2.09% |

Lookup picks the most specific band that exists: platform + niche, else platform, else global. Niche is free text, so it is normalized (lowercased, whitespace collapsed) and only trusted at 40+ samples; otherwise it falls back to the platform band. This is exactly the niche and platform relativity BL-597 called for: a 5% like-rate is normal on TikTok and a strong outlier for Instagram Facts, and the bands encode that difference.

### How it fires, and the small-sample handling (active from zero views)

There is NO view floor that switches the signal off. Instead, tiny counts are made safe statistically:

* **HIGH (over-engagement, the owner's case).** Fires when the clip has at least 8 likes in ABSOLUTE terms AND at least 3x the peer-expected likes (peer median rate times views) AND its rate is above the peer p90. The absolute like floor is what separates a real signal from noise: a brand-new 3-views / 1-like clip has too few likes to mean anything, so it never fires, while a 70-views / 11-likes clip has enough likes to be judged and does. Confidence scales with the absolute like count and drives the sort.
* **LOW (bought views that bring no likes).** Keeps a view floor (2000), because a low like count is only meaningful once a clip has real view volume. Suppressed on YouTube.
* **YouTube is suppressed in both directions.** BL-507 measured that only ~40% of YouTube view-bearing snapshots carry likes (Shorts hide them), so the YouTube band is deflated toward zero and any clip that does report likes reads as falsely over-engaged. Suppressing YT matches how the existing shadow scorer already drops its ratio signal there.

### Unit proof (from `scripts/bl599-verify.ts`)

| case | result |
|---|---|
| owner 70 views / 11 likes (TikTok) | **FIRES HIGH, 3.8x above the TikTok band** (15.7% vs 4.2% median, top 11.9%) |
| 3 views / 1 like | does NOT fire (under the 8-like floor) |
| 20 views / 3 likes | does NOT fire (under the 8-like floor) |
| 500k views / 12k likes (2.4%) | does NOT fire HIGH (a genuinely engaged viral clip is left alone) |
| 300k views / 300 likes (0.1%) | fires LOW (bought-views shape) |
| Instagram Facts, 5000 views / 120 likes (2.4%) | fires HIGH 6.0x, via the niche band (0.4% median) |
| 0 views / 0 likes | does NOT fire (evaluated, but nothing to judge) |

## PART 2: SURFACED AS A RANK, NEVER A VERDICT

The owner-only fraud-review queue (`/api/admin/fraud-review`, `requireOwner`) now carries the signal on every row and adds a third list. All of it is read-only: the handler only reads (`findMany`) and calls pure functions.

* **A new "Engagement vs peers" column** on both existing tables shows the clip's own numbers next to its band in one glance, for example "11 likes / 70 views = 15.7% • above normal 4.2%, top 11.9% (platform)". When the signal did not fire the cell reads "Within normal range" (never a blank cell).
* **The plain reason** is merged into the existing reasons list, for example "Engagement ratio 3.8x above the normal band for this platform: 11 likes on 70 views is 15.7%, where similar clips sit near 4.2%. Too many likes for the views can mean bought likes or an engagement pod."
* **A new "Early signals (not yet flagged)" section** lists APPROVED, still-earning clips the peer signal flags that the old scorer NEVER marked. This is the BL-597 blind half made visible. It is informational only: no action buttons, the owner opens the clip to judge it.
* **The rank.** The peer signal can lift a CLEAN row to "Worth a look" (REVIEW) and nudge its sort position, but it can NEVER, on its own, reach the top FRAUD band (that still needs the shadow scorer's two corroborating strong signals). A single noisy ratio can therefore never present as high-confidence fraud.

Accessibility: the lead reviewed the page and gave GO. Applied: an sr-only `<caption>` on the new table, a unique heading id (`bl510-early-h`) wired to the new section, and `aria-atomic` on the summary status region so the added count announces coherently. ## PART 3: FALSE-POSITIVE GUARD (measured)

There is no ground-truth label of bought versus honest, so this does NOT claim a precision figure (R-5's discipline: do not claim accuracy that has not been proven). What IS measurable is the review LOAD, the honest false-positive proxy:

* Of **2707** live APPROVED clips evaluated, **87 surface (3.2%)**: 63 HIGH (2.3%, of which 33 have under 500 views) and 24 LOW (0.9%). This is a small, readable pile, not a mass flag.
* By inspection, the low-view HIGH fires are dominated by likes approaching or exceeding views, which is genuinely anomalous, not honest noise. The main false-positive vector is a mislabeled or thin niche making a plausible rate look extreme. Because the signal only ranks, such a case costs the owner a few seconds of reading, never a clipper's money.

Even a maximally-suspicious clip is only sorted to the top of the human queue. Nothing is actioned automatically.

### Five real clips it ranks high, judged by inspection

| clip | platform / niche | views / likes | rate | inspection verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cmq52xbip | TikTok / general | 11 / 53 | 482% | **Suspicious.** 53 likes on 11 views is impossible organically. Was invisible before (never marked, low view). |
| cmq6ftzd5 | TikTok / general | 2 / 12 | 600% | **Suspicious or a stale snapshot.** 12 likes on 2 views. Either way worth a human glance; ranks near the top, never auto-acted. |
| cmr504yf3 | Instagram / Comedy, memes | 9911 / 403 | 4.1% | **Not obviously bought.** 4.1% is a plausible real rate; the "comedy, memes" niche median (0.07%) is likely mislabeled or thin. The false-positive-shaped case, correctly only ranked, still earning $4.82 untouched. |
| cmrf0xqxy | Instagram / Comedy, memes | 44477 / 4885 | 11.0% | **Worth a look.** 11% on 44k views is high for the niche; could be genuine virality or a pod. The old scorer had already marked it; the peer signal corroborates. |
| cmqm36vdo | TikTok / Clipping | 5 / 104 | 2080% | **Suspicious.** 104 likes on 5 views. Already bot-marked; the peer signal independently agrees. |

Three are clearly anomalous, one is a corroboration of an existing mark, and one (cmr504yf3) is the honest false-positive-shaped case that proves the rank-not-reject discipline: it is surfaced for judgement and its money is untouched.

## PART 4: PROOF

* **Fires from zero views.** The smallest-view HIGH fires are real clips at 2, 5, 6, 7, 9 and 11 views (see PART 3). The owner's 70 / 11 case fires at 3.8x. The old ratio check, dark below 500 views, saw none of these.
* **A viral clip is only ranked, never rejected.** 500k views / 12k likes (2.4%) does not fire HIGH at all. Nothing in this feature can reject or freeze any clip: the entire path is `findMany` plus pure scoring, returning JSON to an owner-only page.
* **No status or earnings changed.** The signal is computed on the fly in the owner API and never written to the clip or any table. The 6 money files are byte-identical by blob OID against `3e35954a` (`clip-earnings-writer.ts` 7aa6be48, `earnings-calc.ts` 797e2098, `balance.ts` f8499e18, `tracking.ts` b311323c, `clip-earnings-invariant-middleware.ts` 61cef393, `money-decimal.ts` ef5cdae7). `tracking.ts` and `campaign-era.ts` are not in the diff.
* **The clipper sees nothing.** The page is owner-only (`requireOwner` on the API, an OWNER role guard on the page), and `clip-sanitize.ts` still strips every fraud field from clipper responses (unchanged). The peer signal is never attached to any clipper-facing route.
