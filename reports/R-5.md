# R-5: Where the owner's time actually goes

Measured 2026-07-17 from live prod. Window: 30 days unless stated. Read only, nothing built or enabled.
Method: owner hours are **sessionized** from real timestamps (consecutive actions ≤10min apart = one working
session, sum the real gaps). This measures wall clock at the console, not a guessed minutes per task.

## Verdict first

**The owner's instinct is right. Clip review is the top drain, by 10x over the next item.** It is 14.9 of
19.6 measurable owner hours per month (76%).

**But the conclusion that follows is the opposite of "build clip review automation."** Review is already
52.6% delegated to a REVIEWER who costs no engineering at all, and the one signal that could gate an
auto-approve (`fraudScore`) is measurably worthless. The 14.9 hours are already recoverable with config,
not code.

## PART 1: the real operation (30 days)

2302 clips submitted, 1938 approved, 343 rejected (15.0%), 89 owner overrides. 127 distinct clippers
(49 in the last 7 days). 10 campaigns touched, 5 active, 2 created. ~65 payout actions, ~$3.4k paid.
Queue is healthy: 17 pending of 1150, median 9.2h to decision. Nobody is under water.

**Owner hours per month, ranked** (audit_logs, passive/system events excluded):

| Task | Volume | Owner hrs/mo | Share |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Clip review** | 1130 decisions | **14.91** | **76%** |
| Owner override submit | 89 | 1.47 | 7% |
| Payouts | 69 actions | 0.98 | 5% |
| Other admin | 75 | 0.92 | 5% |
| Force/tracking nudge | 77 | 0.90 | 5% |
| Campaign admin | 27 | 0.44 | 2% |
| **Total** | | **19.62** | |

Cross checked independently against `clips.reviewedAt` (different table, same answer): owner review =
15.5 h/mo, median 25 seconds per decision. The two methods agree, so the 14.9 is trustworthy.

**Review is already half delegated.** One active REVIEWER does 1206 decisions in 17.0 h/mo = **52.6%**.
Owner accounts do 47.4%. Total review labour is 32.5 h/mo; the owner carries 46% of it by choice.
(Clipper handles are redacted throughout: this repo is public and BL-539 set that precedent.)

**Testing the BL-539 "99 overrides" cost.** Confirmed 99 all time, 89 in the last 30 days, so it is
accelerating. But it is **not 99 favours for 99 people: it is 11 users**, and one user took 37 in two days
(a bulk backfill episode, not a recurring tax). Priced honestly it is 1.47 h/mo, **10x smaller than review**.
`overrideReason` is null on 94 of 99, so the field is not carrying its purpose.

## PART 2: manual work nobody counted

**The big one: account verification silently fails half the time.** 617 verify attempts produced **326
NEEDS_ADMIN escalations from 114 distinct clippers** in 30 days. TikTok is worst (212 escalations, and
`cappedAtTier1` shows it has no tier 2 fallback); YouTube 108. This is ~4x the override volume and nobody
has counted it.
**Honest limit: I cannot price it.** There is no audit action for a manual account approval, and 237 of the
stuck accounts later reached APPROVED with no admin event, so most self resolve by retrying. The cost lands
as Discord DMs, which are outside the database. It is a large *user facing* failure of unproven owner cost.
The link is visible though: one override reason reads "cant verify app was not working".

**Support is not being answered where it is filed.** 41 open support tickets vs 8 resolved; only 12 clipper
messages in 30 days and zero owner replies in app. Support has migrated to Discord and is unmeasurable here.

**Free text rules force manual review, permanently.** `requirements`, `bannedContent`, `captionRules`,
`hashtagRules`, `aiKnowledge` are all `String? @db.Text`, validated for **length only** (5000 chars).
No code path reads them at review time; they are write only display strings. `targetAudience` (4 value enum),
`videoLengthMin/Max` and `minViews` are the only machine checkable rules. **This is the root cause: while a
rule is prose, a human must read every clip against it.** Rejection reasons confirm it, being long prose
essays about logo placement and card colours.

**Resolved, not a drain (reported for completeness).** 110 `prisma.user.update()` schema drift errors on
`/api/user/pwa-status`, but all confined to July 4 and 5, one user, none since. My first sessionizer wrongly
credited these 4.71 hours; they are background requests firing during owner sessions, not owner labour.
Correcting that was necessary to keep the ranking honest.

Also unenforced: campaign `endDate` is never compared to `now` anywhere, and no code writes `COMPLETED`.
Campaigns only ever stop by hitting budget. No live instances today, so cost is currently zero.

## PART 3: built but dark

**Growth engine: live, and it has reached exactly one person.** `growth_first_clip` fired **1 time in 30
days**. Only 5 `first_clip` events exist at all (field added ~July 13). Current value is approximately zero.

**Correcting the brief on two points.**
1. **BL-524 does not recommend CLIP_APPROVED_POST_AGAIN.** BL-524 is about a 97 minute cron stall. The
   recommendation is in `docs/GOLIVE-CHECKLIST.md:243`, where it is framed as an *alternative to*
   FIRST_CLIP_CELEBRATE (zero email risk but tests no email), not a successor.
2. **"~528 clippers a week" is wrong by ~10x.** 528 is the *approved clips* per week (509, 589 measured).
   Distinct clippers with an approval per week is **48 to 50**. Firing per clip would mean ~11 nudges per
   person per week, into an audience of 50, who **already receive CLIP_APPROVED** (1931 sent, 1719 read).
   It duplicates a notification that already exists and already works. Risk is spam, not deliverability.

Hidden gate worth knowing: 11 of the 16 triggers are poll sourced and need `RAILWAY_NATIVE_CRON` to include
`lifecycle`. `vercel.json` schedules the cron but hosting is Railway, so they would silently no op.

**Fraud queue: built, ranked, worked zero times, and the ranking does not work.** 2201 clips scored,
439 above zero, **0 ever dismissed**. Critically, **`fraudScore` has no predictive power**:

| fraudScore | clips | reject rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 2920 | **19.0%** |
| 1 to 19 | 402 | 7.3% |
| 20 to 39 | 316 | 11.4% |
| 40+ | 82 | **19.5%** |

Score 0 clips are rejected at the same rate as score 40+ clips. **This kills auto-approve on the existing
signal**: gating on low scores would wrongly auto-approve 19% of clips straight into the money path.

**Marketplace: 0 submissions, 0 earnings, 1 listing, 1 application.** Never taken money. Switch is
`NEXT_PUBLIC_MARKETPLACE_ENABLED=true`, needs a rebuild (build time inlined). It is a revenue bet, not a
time saver, so it does not compete on this list.

**Not the automation they sound like:** `proposed_clip_decisions` *adds* an owner ratification click
(9 ever, 7 declined). `creator_scans` is not in the Prisma schema, is gated by a capability the owner
cannot grant in the UI, and needs hand typed retention figures. Both flip sign in a time model.

## PART 4: the ranked answer

| # | Automatable task | Hrs saved/mo | Build cost | Risk | Pieces exist? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Shift review to reviewers (scope gap + capacity)** | **up to 14.9** | **zero (config)** | **low** | **yes, already carries 52.6%** |
| 2 | Fix TikTok/YouTube verify cascade (114 stuck/mo) | unpriced, likely high | small | low | partly (tier 2 exists for YT) |
| 3 | Soften 30min window, or use existing bulk submit (30/paste) | 1.5 | zero to small | med (views baseline) | yes, bulk route built |
| 4 | Auto reject dead links (~18% of rejections) | 0.3 | small | low | yes (`videoUnavailable`) |
| 5 | Payout bulk approve | 0.6 | small | **high (money)** | no, `PAYOUT_APPROVE` unwired |
| 6 | Campaign endDate auto complete | ~0 | small | low | no |
| 7 | Turn on more growth triggers | **0** | env var | med (spam) | yes, saves no owner time |
| 8 | Marketplace go live | **0** | env + rebuild | high | yes, revenue not time |
| 9 | Work the fraud queue | **negative** | zero | low | yes, but ranking is worthless |
| 10 | AI auto approve | up to 14.9 | **large** | **very high** | **no, and fraudScore proven useless** |
| 11 | Structured campaign rules (unblocks 10) | 0 alone | large | med | no |

**Is clip review the top? Yes.** 76% of owner console time, 10x the next item. The instinct is correct and
the data backs it.

**But the duller answer beats the exciting one.** Rows 1 and 10 save the same 14.9 hours. Row 10 is a large
build on the money path, and the only signal it could use is measurably worthless. Row 1 is **already built,
already running, already absorbing more than half the load, and costs zero engineering.** Building AI review
would spend months to buy what a config change and a second reviewer buy this week.

The scope gap is concrete: BAD BITCH ANTHEM (x2), Deja Shoe and CROCS have **no reviewer scoped**, forcing
85 owner clips/mo. The other 1045 are in campaigns the reviewer **already covers** (STRAENGE 167 owner vs 8
reviewer; Panic Baby 129 vs 18). So the constraint is reviewer **capacity and habit, not tooling**. The
owner is voluntarily double manning a queue that is already covered and already clear.

## Single highest value next action

**Add the 4 unscoped campaigns to the existing reviewer's scope, and add a second reviewer to cover STRAENGE
and Panic Baby.** Zero code, zero build, zero money risk, reversible in one click. Recovers up to 14.9 owner
hours per month, which is 76% of the measured load, using machinery that already exists and is already
proven at 52.6% of live volume.

Do this before building anything. If review must be automated later, the prerequisite is not a model: it is
**row 11**, turning campaign rules from prose into checkable fields. Until then, no automation can read the
rules it would have to enforce.
